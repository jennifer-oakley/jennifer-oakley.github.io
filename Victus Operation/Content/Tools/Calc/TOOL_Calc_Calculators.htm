About simple calculators

The Tools > Calculators > Calculators tab contains several simple calculators. Refer to the sections below for more information about specific calculators.

  • The Annular Capacity calculator calculates the volume of fluid that is contained in one foot of the annulus. See "Annular capacity".
  • The Annular Velocity calculator calculates the velocity of the fluid in the annulus. See "Annular velocity".
  • The Buoyancy Factor calculator calculates the effect of the buoyancy of the drilling fluid on the weight of the drillstring. See "Buoyancy factor".
  • The Slug calculator calculates the weight or volume of the slug required to achieve the desired length of dry pipe. See "Slug weight or volume".
  • The Hydrostatic Pressure Drop While POOH calculator calculates the hydrostatic pressure loss that occurs when pulling out of hole. See "Hydrostatic pressure drop while POOH".
  • The Stuck Pipe calculator calculates the depth of the point in the well where stuck pipe occurs. See "Stuck pipe depth".
  • The Spotting Pill calculator calculates the volume and pump strokes required for a spotting pill. See "Spotting pill".
  • The Reservoir Inflow Performance calculator calculates the density of the formation fluids and the flow rate at which they flow into the well. See "Reservoir inflow performance".
  • The Pressure Loss calculator calculates the pressure losses and flow regimes in the drillstring and the annulus. See "Pressure loss".
  • The Z Factor calculator calculates the compressibility of a gas in the reservoir. See "Z factor".
  • The Gas Solubility calculator calculates the amount of gas that will dissolve in oil at the chokes and at the flowmeter. See "Gas solubility".
  • The Weight Up Mud calculator calculates the amount of weighting material, such as barite or calcium carbonate, needed to increase the density of the current drilling fluid. See "Weight up mud".
  • The Round Trip calculator calculates the amount of work required to pull the drillstring completely out of well and running it back in the hole. See "Round trip ton-miles".
  • The Drilling or Connection calculator calculates the amount of work required to drill down the length of a section of drill pipe. See "Drilling or connection ton-miles".
  • The Coring calculator calculates the amount of work required for a coring operation. See "Coring ton-miles".
  • The Setting Casing calculator calculates the amount of work required to set a casing string. See "Setting casing ton-miles".
  • The Short Trip calculator calculates the amount of work required for a short trip. See "Short trip ton-miles".
  • The Pump Output calculator calculates the volume of each pump stroke. See "Pump output".
  • The Pump Relation calculator calculates the change in circulating pressure that results when the pump rate changes. See "Pump relation".
  • The Volume Distribution calculator calculates the distribution of the fluid and steel volume throughout the well, as well as the total hole volume. See "Volume distribution".

Annular capacity

The annular capacity calculator is used to calculate the volume of fluid that is in one foot of the annulus. The table below defines the fields for the annular capacity calculator.

Field definitions for the annular capacity calculator
Field Name Definition
Casing ID Inside diameter of the smallest casing section in the current well interval
Pipe OD Outside diameter of the pipe in the current drillstring
Annular Capacity, bbl/ft Volume of fluid inside one foot of the annulus. This value is calculated from the casing ID and the pipe OD.
Annular Capacity, ft/bbl Length of the annulus that is occupied by one barrel of fluid. This value is calculated from the casing ID and the pipe OD.

Annular velocity

The annular velocity calculator is used to calculate the velocity of the fluid in the annulus. There are two methods available for calculating annular velocity.

  • Formula 1 calculates annular velocity based on pump output and annular capacity.
  • Formula 2 calculates annular velocity based on flow rate, using an integrated calculator for annular capacity.

The table below defines the fields for the annular velocity calculator.

Field definitions for the annular velocity calculator
Calculation Method Field Name Definition
Formula 1 Pump Output Total flow rate of the fluid
Annular Capacity Volume of fluid inside one foot of the annulus. See "Annular capacity".
Annular Velocity Velocity of the fluid in the annulus. This value is calculated from the pump output and annular capacity.
Formula 2 Flow Rate Flow rate of the fluid in the annulus
Bigger Diameter Inside diameter of the smallest casing section in the current well interval
Smaller Diameter Outside diameter of the pipe in the current drillstring
Annular Velocity Velocity of the fluid in the annulus. This value is calculated from the flow rate and the diameters.

Buoyancy factor

The buoyancy factor calculator is used to calculate the effect of the buoyancy of the drilling fluid on the weight of the drillstring. The buoyancy factor is used to determine the amount of weight lost due to the immersion of the drillstring in the drilling fluid. The calculated buoyancy factor is used to calculate the weight of the drillstring in mud. To calculate the weight of the drillstring in mud, multiply the weight of the drillstring in air by the calculated buoyancy factor.

The table below defines the fields for the buoyancy factor calculator.

Field definitions for the buoyancy factor calculator
Field Name Definition
Mud Weight Density of the current drilling fluid
Buoyancy Factor Value representing the effect of the buoyancy of the drilling fluid on the weight of the drillstring. This value is calculated based on the mud weight.

Slug weight or volume

The slug calculator is used to calculate the weight or volume of the slug required to achieve the desired length of dry pipe. Slug is heavy mud, which is used to push down lighter mud before pulling drill pipe out of the well. Slug is often used when the drill pipe becomes wet while pulling it out of the well.

The slug calculator can be used to calculate either slug weight or slug volume. When one of these values is entered, the other value will be automatically calculated.

The table below defines the fields for the slug calculator.

Field definitions for the slug calculator
Field Name Definition
Dry Pipe Length Desired length of dry drill pipe
Pipe Capacity Volume of fluid contained inside one foot of the drill pipe
Mud Weight Density of the current drilling fluid
Slug Weight Density of the slug fluid. When slug volume is entered, this is a calculated value.
Slug Volume Volume of the slug fluid. When slug weight is entered, this is a calculated value.

Hydrostatic pressure drop while POOH

The calculator for hydrostatic pressure drop while POOH pulling out of hole is used to calculate the hydrostatic pressure loss that occurs when pulling out of hole. There are two methods available for calculating the resulting hydrostatic pressure loss:

  • The When Pulling Dry Pipe method should be used when you will be pulling dry pipe out of the hole. This method assumes that there is no drilling fluid inside the drill pipe, and only takes into account the volume of the pipe that will be pulled out of the hole.
  • When Pulling Wet Pipe method should be used when you will be pulling wet pipe out of the hole. This method assumes that there is drill fluid inside the drill pipe, and takes into account the volume of the pipe and the volume of the mud inside the pipe.

The table below defines the fields for the hydrostatic pressure drop while POOH calculator.

Field definitions for the hydrostatic pressure drop while POOH calculator
Calculation Method Field Name Definition
When Pulling Dry Pipe No. Of Stands Pulled Number of drill pipe stands that will be pulled out of the hole
Length Per Stand Average length of each drill pipe stand
Pipe Displacement Volume of fluid displaced by one foot of drill pipe
Casing Capacity Volume of fluid contained inside one foot of casing
Mud Weight Density of the current drilling fluid
Hydrostatic Pressure Drop Hydrostatic pressure loss that results when pulling dry pipe out of the hole. This value is calculated from the total pipe volume, mud weight, and casing capacity.
When Pulling Wet Pipe % Volume in Drill Pipe Percentage of the volume inside the drill pipe that was filled with fluid before pulling out of hole
No. Of Stands Pulled Number of drill pipe stands that will be pulled out of the hole
Length Per Stand Average length of each drill pipe stand
Pipe Displacement Volume of fluid displaced by one foot of drill pipe
Pipe Capacity Volume of fluid contained inside one foot of drill pipe
Casing Capacity Volume of fluid contained inside one foot of casing
Mud Weight Density of the current drilling fluid
Hydrostatic Pressure Drop Hydrostatic pressure loss that results when pulling wet pipe out of the hole. This value is calculated automatically.

Stuck pipe depth

The stuck pipe calculator is used to calculate the depth of the point in the well where stuck pipe occurs. The table below defines the fields for the stuck pipe calculator.

Field definitions for the stuck pipe calculator
Field Name Definition
Stretch Amount that the drillstring stretches when the amount of WOB is increased
Pull Force Amount by which WOB increased
Pipe Outer Diameter Outside diameter of the drill pipe, used to calculate pipe weight
Pipe Inner Diameter Inside diameter of the drill pipe, used to calculate pipe weight
Depth of Stuck Pipe Depth of the point in the well where stuck pipe occurs. This value is calculated from drill pipe stretch, pull force, and pipe weight.

Spotting pill

The spotting pill calculator is used to calculate the volume and pump strokes required for a spotting pill. A spotting pill is a small volume of fluid placed in the annulus to free differentially stuck pipe.

The table below defines the fields for the spotting pill calculator.

Field definitions for the spotting pill calculator
Field Name Definition
Hole Diameter Diameter of the open hole section in the current well interval
Washout Factor Percentage by which the wellbore is enlarged in the washed out section of the well
Drill Pipe OD Outside diameter of the drill pipe
Drill Pipe Capacity Volume of fluid contained inside one foot of drill pipe
Drill Pipe Length Total length of the drill pipe in the well
Drill Collar OD Outside diameter of the drill collars
Drill Collar Capacity Volume of fluid contained inside one foot of drill collar
Drill Collar Length Total length of the drill collars in the well
Pump Output Total flow rate of the fluid
Surface System Total Strokes Strokes required to displace surface system from suction tank to the drill pipe
Pill Around Drill Pipe Height of the spotting pill in the annulus
Pill In Drill Pipe Volume of the spotting pill inside the drill pipe
Total Pill Volume Required for Spotting Pill Total fluid volume required to pump the pill into the well and circulate it out of well. This value is calculated automatically.
Strokes Required to Spot Pill Total pump strokes required to pump the pill into the well and circulate it out of well. This value is calculated automatically.

The reservoir inflow performance calculator is used to calculate the density of the formation fluids and the flow rate at which they flow into the well.

The table below defines the fields for the reservoir inflow performance calculator.

Field definitions for the reservoir inflow performance calculator
Field Name Definition
Pore Pressure Pore pressure of the formation
Bottom Hole Pressure Current bottomhole pressure
Bottom Hole Temperature Current bottomhole temperature
Height of Flowing Reservoir Section Height of the formation section in which formation fluids are flowing into the well
Permeability Permeability of the formation
Gas Gravity Specific gravity of the gases in the reservoir, at standard temperature and pressure
Reservoir Radius Distance from the wellbore wall at which pore pressure becomes constant
Hole Diameter Open hole diameter in the current well interval
Gas Flow Rate Rate at which gases are flowing into the formation. If pore pressure is less than bottomhole pressure, this value will be zero. This value is calculated automatically.
Gas Density Density of the gases in the formation. This value is calculated from the pore pressure of the formation.

The weight up mud calculator is used to calculate the amount of weighting material, such as barite or calcium carbonate, needed to increase the density of the current drilling fluid.

The table below defines the fields for the weight up mud calculator.

Field definitions for the weight up mud calculator
Field Name Definition
Fluid Density Current mud weight
Weight-Up Density Target mud weight
Weighting Material Density Density of the material used to increase mud weight (e.g. barite, calcium carbonate, hematite)
Mud Tank Volume Current volume of fluid in the mud tank
Lbs of Weighting Material per Sack Weight of each sack of weighting material

Pump relation

The pump relation calculator is used to calculate the change in circulating pressure that results when the pump rate changes. There are two methods available for calculating the new circulating pressure:

  • The Basic Calculation is a simple formula that uses a standard factor to represent the relationship between pump pressure readings.
  • The Advanced Calculation is a more accurate formula that calculates the relationship between pump readings.

The table below defines the fields for the pump relation calculator.

Field definitions for the pump relation calculator
Calculation Method Field Name Definition
Basic Calculation Current Circulating Pressure Circulating pressure at the previous pump rate
Old Pump Rate Previous pump rate, before adjusting the pump strokes
New Pump Rate New pump rate, after adjusting the pump strokes
New Circulating Pressure Circulating pressure at the new pump rate. This value is calculated automatically.
Advanced Calculation Pressure 1 First pressure reading
Flow Rate 1 Flow rate when the first pressure reading was measured
Pressure 2 Second pressure reading
Flow Rate 2 Flow rate when the second pressure reading was measured
Factor Factor representing the relationship between pump readings. This value is calculated automatically.
Current Circulating Pressure Circulating pressure at the previous pump rate
Old Pump Rate Previous pump rate, before adjusting the pump strokes
New Pump Rate New pump rate, after adjusting the pump strokes
New Circulating Pressure Circulating pressure at the new pump rate. This value is calculated automatically.
