About pressure limits

Pressure limits are used to control the maximum and minimum values for surface backpressure. The high limit helps prevent excessive pressure in the wellbore during MPD operations. In addition to the high limit, a low limit is calculated to prevent pressure from dropping below the formation resistance. Victus™ Operation calculates the high limit and low limit automatically. You can also manually override the calculated high limit with another value. See "About the high limit".

About the high limit

Victus™ Operation's behavior regarding the high limit is configured in the Setup > Control Algorithms > Limits tab. According to the settings in the Limits tab, when 70%, 80% or 90% of the high limit is reached, Victus™ Operation will update the SBP set point and keep it at the selected percentage of the high limit. See "Limits algorithm".

When surface backpressure reaches 98% of the high limit:

  • If you selected the Fully Open Chokes to 100% option, Victus™ Operation will change the MPD control mode to None mode. Any chokes that are disabled when an emergency shutdown occurs will be activated and fully opened. If a choke is in calibration mode, it will not open when surface backpressure reaches 98% of the high limit. The choke will only open when it is out of calibration mode.
  • If you selected the Go to Low Limit (based on Pore Pressure) - SBP mode option, Victus™ Operation will update the SBP to the low limit value. See "About the low limit".

The calculated high limit is the lesser of the following values:

  • RCD pressure rating
  • Riser pressure rating
  • Maximum allowable annular surface pressure

If the high limit cannot be calculated, or the calculated high limit is zero, the high limit will be set to the previous value. If there is no previous value, the high limit will be set to 5000 psi.

The pressure ratings for the RCD and riser can be updated on the Setup > Surface Equipment > Piping and Instrumentation tab. See "Equipment specifications". Maximum allowable annular surface pressure is calculated using the following formula:

  • MAASP = CSD x min(FIT,FPG) x 0.052 – AP + SBP,
  • where
    • MAASP is the maximum allowable annular surface pressure (psi)
    • CSD is the casing shoe depth (ft)
    • FIT is the results from the formation integrity test (ppg)
    • FPG is the fracture pressure gradient
    • AP is the annular pressure at the casing shoe
    • SBP is the surface backpressure

When you click the High Limit tile, the High Limit window will open. The High Limit window shows two values:

  • Pressure High Limit – This is the calculated high limit. If you reset the high limit from an override value, this is the value that will be used for the new high limit. See "Resetting the high limit".
  • Overwrite High Limit – This is the current high limit. If you are using the calculated high limit, this value will match the value in the Pressure High Limit field. If not, this value is current override value. See "Overriding the high limit".

Overriding the high limit

To override the calculated high limit:

  1. In the Well View pane, click the High Limit tile.
  2. In the High Limit window, enter the high limit in the Overwrite High Limit field.
  3. Click the Enter button (), the click Yes on the confirmation message that appears.
  4. Click the Close button () to close the window.

The high limit shown on the High Limit tile will be updated to the new value. The text color in the High Limit tile will change to orange to indicate that a manual value has been entered.

Resetting the high limit

If you previously entered a value to override the calculated high limit, you can reset the high limit to the calculated value. To reset the high limit:

  1. In the Well View pane, click the High Limit tile.
  2. In the High Limit window, click the Undo button ().
  3. Click Yes on the confirmation message that appears.
  4. Click the Close button () to close the window.

The high limit will be reset to the calculated value shown in the Pressure High Limit field. The text color in the High Limit tile will change to white to indicate that the calculated value is being used.

About the low limit

In most cases, the pressure low limit is calculated using the following formula:

LL = SBP - (smallest difference between PP and AP)

Where:

  • SBP is the surface backpressure
  • PP is the pore pressure gradient
  • AP is the annular pressure

However, if pore pressure exceeds annular pressure, the following formula is used:

LL = SBP + (difference between PP and AP)

The maximum value of the low limit is determined by the option selected in the System goes to SBP mode at field on the Setup > Control Algorithms > Limits tab. The low limit cannot exceed the percentage of the high limit at which the system switches to SBP mode.

If it exceeds that value, the low limit will set to the percentage of the high limit. For example, if the high limit is 1000 psi and the 70% of High Limit option is selected, the low limit will be set to 700 psi. See "Limits algorithm". The current low limit value is displayed on the chart in the Main Time Data dashboard in addition to the high limit.

Note

The complete functionality for the pressure low limit only works in Victus™ Operation. In Victus™ Simulation, the low limit value shows on the Home screen. But the system does not maintain the pressure when it approaches the high limit.
