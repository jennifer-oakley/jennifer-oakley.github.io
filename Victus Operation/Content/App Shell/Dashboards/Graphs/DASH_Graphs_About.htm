Using graphs on the Home screen

About graphs

Curve data can be plotted in graphs on the Home screen. Each graph can contain multiple tracks, and each track can contain multiple curves. All of the curves in a graph use the same index. There are several types of graphs:

  • Time graphs are indexed based on time, along a vertical axis. See "Time graphs".
  • Depth graphs are indexed based on measured depth, along either a vertical or horizontal axis. See "Depth graphs".
  • Cross plots are indexed on any time-based parameter, along either a vertical or horizontal axis. See "Cross plots".
  • Toolface plots show the direction that the bit is currently pointing. See "Toolface plots".

Using graphs on the Home screen

There are several keyboard shortcuts and mouse actions that you can use to manipulate the graphs on the Home screen.

  • To scroll to a different point in the graph index, hover over a graph and use the scroll wheel on your mouse. To return to the current graph values, click on the graph with the scroll wheel.
  • To toggle between legend display modes, hover your mouse over the top edge of a vertical graph or the left edge of a horizontal graph, then click on the bar that appears.
  • To expand the size of a graph track, double-click on the track.
  • To change the scale of the graph index, press the Ctrl key and use the scroll wheel to zoom in and out.
  • To change the scale of the curve axis, press the Shift key and use the scroll wheel to zoom in and out. To return to the original scale, click on the graph with the scroll wheel.
  • To view the exact values for a specific point in all curves in a track, press the Shift key and hover your mouse over the graph.
  • To view the exact values for a specific point in all curves in all tracks, press Ctrl + Shift and hover your mouse over the graph.
  • To change the settings for a graph, press the Alt key and click on the graph.
