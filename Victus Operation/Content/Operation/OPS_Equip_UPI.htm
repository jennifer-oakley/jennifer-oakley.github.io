Victus™ MPD UPI

Related Topics

The Victus™ UPI universal peripheral interface is a specialized network switch that connects to the IU instrumentation unit on the MPD manifold. It sends information received from the IU to the workstations connected to it. It can also send and receive WITS data and analog sensor data.

Diagram of the ports on the UPI

The diagram above shows the ports on the UPI. There are six types of ports on the UPI:

Ports on the UPI
Port Type Description
LAN ports Standard network ports that connect MPD workstations to the UPI. There are eight LAN ports, which require a standard network cable to connect to the UPI.
Wi-Fi switch Turns the UPI's internal wireless router on and off
WAN ports

Standard network ports that connect the UPI to external networks. There are two WAN ports, which require a standard network cable to connect to the UPI.

  • The WAN-1 port provides internet access and VPN access.
  • The WAN-2 port connects to the local rig network.
Serial ports

Connects the UPI to EDR WITS machines. There are three serial ports:

  • The WITS (Serial 1) and RS232-4 (Serial 2) ports can be used to send or receive WITS data, depending on the WITS configuration in Victus™ Operation. See "WITS data". The WITS (Serial 1) port requires a straight serial cable to connect to the UPI, and the RS232-4 (Serial 2) port requires a null modem serial cable.
  • The RS232-3 (WITS Out) port can only be used to send outgoing WITS data. The RS232-3 (WITS Out) port requires a straight serial cable to connect to the UPI.
Analog sensor ports Three-wire female terminals that connect up to eight standard 4-20 mA analog sensors to the UPI
Fiber optic ports Connects the UPI to the Azonix panel and the IU or ICU. There are four fiber optic ports, which require a fiber optic cable that's rated for hazardous environments.
