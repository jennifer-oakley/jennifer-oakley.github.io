About basic properties

When you create a new fluid , the basic fluid properties are entered in the top portion of the screen. The basic fluid properties include fluid type, fluid weight, temperatures, and the fluid composition. If the fluid report includes additional information, such as salt type and salinity, you can enter this information on the right side of the screen. If not, you can simply use the default values.

Adding fluids

To add a drilling mud:

  1. Go to the Setup > Fluid Properties page.
  2. Click the Add button () at the bottom of the screen. A new numbered tab will appear in the toolbar to the right.
  3. In the Fluid Type field, select the type of fluid you want to add.
  4. In the Fluid Weight In field, enter the density of the fluid when it enters the wellbore.
  5. In the Fluid Inlet Temperature field, enter the inlet temperature of the mud in the suction tank.
  6. In the Ref. Temperature field, enter the temperature at which the mud weight was measured.
  7. Enter the composition of the mud in the Retort Oil and Retort Water fields.
  8. If available, enter the remaining information. See "Field definitions".
  9. Enter the isothermal or PVT dial readings. See "Isothermal dial readings" and "PVT dial readings".
  10. Click the Save Changes button () at the bottom of the screen.

The fluid will be stored in Victus™ Operation's database. When you change the pump fluid, you'll be able to select the new fluid under the Mud utility. However, you will not be able to edit the fluid properties or dial readings.

Tip

After you save the fluid, you can use the Mud button () in the utility bar to set the fluid as the pump fluid or boost line fluid. See "Mud utility".

Tip

To change the name of the fluid, go to the toolbar on the right side of the screen and click on the numbered tab for the fluid. In the box that appears, enter a new name for the fluid and press the Enter key.

Field definitions

The table below defines the basic properties for muds on the Setup > Fluid Properties > Fluids tab.

Field definitions for basic fluid properties
Field Definition
Fluid Type

Base fluid used for the mud

  • Mineral Oil Base is a drilling fluid in which the continuous phase is mineral oil.
  • Oil Base is a drilling fluid in which the continuous phase is diesel oil.
  • Synthetic Base is a drilling fluid in which the continuous phase is a synthetic fluid, such as a polyether or a linear alkyl benzene.
  • Water Base is a drilling fluid in which the continuous phase is water or saltwater, such as fresh water, potassium, or silicate.
  • Cement Slurries is a cementing fluid that contains limestone and clay, as well as various types of additives.
  • Spacers is a cementing fluid that are generally water-based Newtonian fluids that contain viscosifiers and other additives.
Fill Color

Color used to represent the fluid in the Well View pane. This is a read-only value, determined by the mud type.
Fluid Weight In

Density of the fluid when it enters the wellbore
Fluid Inlet Temperature Inlet temperature of the fluid in the suction tank
Ref. Temperature Temperature at which the mud weight was measured
Retort Oil Percentage of oil in the fluid when it enters the wellbore. Not available for water-based fluids.
Retort Water Percentage of water in the fluid when it enters the wellbore.
Solids Percentage of solids in the fluid when it enters the wellbore. Read-only value, automatically calculated from the Retort Oil and Retort Water values.
Salt Type Type of salt used in the fluid
Salinity Concentration of the salt in the fluid when it enters the wellbore. Salinity should be entered as a percentage of the weight of the fluid.
Conductivity Conductivity of the fluid. The default value is calculated based on the fluid composition.
Specific Heat Specific heat of the fluid. The default value is calculated based on the fluid composition.
API Fluid Loss Amount of water loss that occurs in 30 minutes at 100 psi. Only available for water-based fluids.
HTHP Fluid Loss Amount of water loss that occurs in 30 minutes at 500 psi. Not available for water-based fluids.
API Cake Thickness of the filter cake. Only available for water-based fluids.
pH Acidity of the fluid. Only available for water-based fluids.
Proppants Type of particles mixed with the fluid
Gas Type Type of gas mixed with the fluid
Gas Density Density of the gas mixed with the fluid, automatically determined by the selected gas type. Not available when the None option is selected in the Gas Type field.
