About rig elevations

The Well View pane on the Setup >Surface Equipment > Equipment Configuration tab shows a diagram that illustrates the kelly bushing, ground level, and water depth of the rig. The diagram shown depends on the type of rig selected in the Rig Type field.

The elevations entered on the Surface Equipment page are used to determine the starting depth of the well, which is the measured depth at the top of the well. The well's starting depth is used for the MD of the first formation layer, as well as for the hanger depth for casing sections. The method for calculating the starting MD depends on the type of rig.

  • For offshore wells, the starting MD is the sum of the kelly bushing elevation and the water depth. See "Offshore rig elevations".
  • For land wells, the starting MD is the difference between the kelly bushing elevation and the ground level elevation. See "Land rig elevations".

You'll also need to enter the elevations of the RCD, choke manifold, and MPD manifold on the rig. These values are used in hydraulics calculations, such as the size of the air gap. See "Equipment elevations".

Offshore rig elevations

To enter the elevations for an offshore rig:

  1. Go to the Equipment Configuration tab on the Setup > Surface Equipment page.
  2. Select the type of rig from the list in the Rig Type field.
  3. In the Kelly Bushing field, enter the distance between the top of the kelly bushing and mean sea level.
  4. In the Water Depth field, enter the distance between the sea floor and mean sea level.
  5. Click the Save Changes button () at the bottom of the screen, then click OK on the warning message that appears.

The well's starting depth will be updated automatically, based on the water depth and the elevation of the kelly bushing. The diagram in the Well View pane will show the new elevations.

Land rig elevations

To enter the elevations for a land rig:

  1. Go to the Equipment Configuration tab on the Setup > Surface Equipment page.
  2. In the Rig Type field, select the Land option.
  3. In the Kelly Bushing field, enter the distance between the top of the kelly bushing and mean sea level.
  4. In the Ground Level field, enter the distance between the ground level and mean sea level.
  5. Click the Save Changes button () at the bottom of the screen, then click OK on the warning message that appears.

The well's starting depth will be updated automatically, based on the ground level and kelly bushing elevations. The diagram in the Well View pane will show the new elevations.

Equipment elevations

To enter the elevation of the RCD, MPD manifold, or choke manifold:

  1. Go to the Piping and Instrumentation tab on the Setup > Surface Equipment page.
  2. Select the RCD, choke manifold, or MPD in the rig schematic. A panel with the equipment properties will appear next to the rig schematic.
  3. In the Elevation field, enter the distance between the bottom of the equipment and the top of the kelly bushing.
    • If the equipment is below the kelly bushing, enter a positive number.
    • If the equipment is above the kelly bushing, enter a negative number.
  4. Click the Save Changes button () to save your changes.

The Victus™ Operation database will be updated with the new equipment elevation.

After you update the equipment elevations, the next step is to enter the rig limits. See "Entering rig limits".
