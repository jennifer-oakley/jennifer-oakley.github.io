About temperature data

The next step is to add formation temperatures to the well. The Temperatures tab on the Setup > Well Data page is used to create a temperature profile of the formation layers in the well. The data is indexed by the TVD of the formation layer, from the surface to the bottom of the well.

The table at the top of the Temperatures tab lists all of the temperature points for the well. Temperature data is graphed on a chart below the table.

If the well is located offshore, the temperature data should include temperature points for the water between the rig and the top of the well. The blue band at the top of the graph is used to identify water temperatures. See "Water temperatures".

There are two modes for entering temperature data:

  • In Temperature mode, you can enter the temperatures measured at each temperature point. The thermal gradients at each temperature point will be automatically calculated from the temperatures.
  • In Gradient mode, you can enter the thermal gradients along the wellbore formation. The temperatures at each point will be automatically calculated from the thermal gradients.

The Temperature Mode button () button at the bottom of the screen allows you to switch between these two modes. When you're in Temperature mode, the heading for the second column in the table will be Temperature. When you're in Gradient mode, the heading will be GG.

Adding temperatures

To add formation temperatures:

  1. Go to the Temperatures tab on the Setup > Well Data page.
  2. If you're in Gradient mode, click the Temperature Mode button () to switch to Temperature mode.
  3. Click the Add button (). A new row will appear at the bottom of the table.
  4. In the TVD column, enter the TVD of the depth where the temperature was measured.
  5. In the Temperature column, enter the temperature for that depth.
  6. When you are finished adding temperatures, click the Save Changes button ().

As you add temperatures to the table, the chart at the bottom of screen will be updated with the new values. When you save your changes, the temperature data will be stored in Victus™ Operation's database.

After you enter the temperature data, the next step is to define the influx zones for the well. See "Defining influx zones".
