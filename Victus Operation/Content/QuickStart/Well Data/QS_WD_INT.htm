About well intervals

The next step is to add the wellbore intervals. The Well Intervals tab on the Setup > Well Data page is used to define the geometry of the hole sections along the trajectory of the well. Casing sections are listed in the top portion of the Well Intervals tab, and open hole sections are listed at the bottom. The Well View pane shows a preview of the wellbore, including the casing sections, liner sections, and open hole sections.

There are two steps for defining the well intervals.

  • Define the open hole sections. See "Defining open hole sections".
  • Define the casing and liner sections. See "Defining casing sections".

Defining open hole sections

To add an open hole section:

  1. Go to the Well Intervals tab on the Setup > Well Data page.
  2. Click the Insert Item button (), then select the OH option in the menu that appears.
  3. Double-click the MD field in the row that appears, then enter the depth at the bottom of open hole section.
  4. Enter the hole size in the Hole Diameter field.
  5. Enter the friction factor for the open hole section in the Friction Factor field.
  6. Click the Save Changes button () at the bottom of the screen.

The new open hole section will be added to the list of open hole sections in the bottom half of the screen. The dimensions of the new open hole section will be appear in the Well View pane.

Defining casing sections

Properties of casing

Properties of liner

To add a casing or liner section:

  1. Go to the Well Intervals tab on the Setup > Well Data page.
  2. Click the Insert Item button (), then select the CS option in the menu that appears.
  3. In the panel that appears on the right, select the type of casing or liner from the drop-down list in the Type field.
  4. Select the section material from the list in the Material field.
  5. Enter the dimensions of the section in the OD, NW, and ID fields.
  6. Enter the depth at the bottom of the section in the SD field.
  7. For liner sections, enter the depth at the top of the section in the HD field.
  8. Click the OK button () at the bottom of the screen to save your changes.

The new section will be added to the list of casing and liner sections in the top half of the screen. The dimensions of the new section will appear in the Well View pane.

After you define the wellbore intervals, the next step is to enter the temperature data. See "Entering temperature data".
