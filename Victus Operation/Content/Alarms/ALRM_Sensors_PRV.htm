PRV alarms

On the PRV tab, red boxes are used to indicate an active alarm for sensors. If a sensor is not shown, it is functioning within specified limits. The PRV tab is only visible when the Weatherford PRV option is selected on the Setup > Surface Equipment > Equipment Configuration tab. See "Equipment configuration".

The table below lists the alarms that can be shown on the Alarms > Sensors > PRV tab:

Alarms on the Alarms > Sensors > PRV tab
Alarm Definition
Power supply 1 fault Power Supply 1 is not producing power. Make sure the power supply is turned on.
Power supply 2 fault Power Supply 2 is not producing power. Make sure the power supply is turned on.
HMI fuse There is no power emanating from the HMI human machine interface fuse (F3).
Fiber Ethernet switch fuse The Ethernet switch that routes communication to Victus™ Operation has a blown fuse (F4).
Copper Ethernet switch fuse The Ethernet switch that routes communications to the HMI has a blown fuse (F5).
PRV 1 barrier fuse Input Fuse 1 (F6) has blown. This will de-energize PLC 1, the PRV 1 proximity sensor, the hydraulic fluid level, and PT5. If PT5 is in service when this alarm occurs, PT5 should be disabled until it can be replaced.
PRV 1 analog fuse Input Fuse 2 (F7) has blown. This will de-energize PT6, air pressure, and the close pressure for PRV 1. If PT6 is in service when this alarm occurs, PT6 should be disabled until it can be replaced.
PRV 2 barrier fuse Input Fuse 3 (F8) has blown. This will de-energize PLC 2, the PRV 2 proximity sensor, and PT7. If PT7 is in service when this alarm occurs, PT7 should be disabled until it can be replaced.
PRV 2 analog fuse Input Fuse 4 (F9) has blown. This will de-energize PT8, hydraulic pressure, and the close pressure for PRV 2. If PT8 is in service when this alarm occurs, PT6 should be disabled until it can be replaced.
PRV 1 open fuse The digital output fuse for PLC 1 (F10) has blown. This will prevent PLC 1 from opening the PRV valves. Replace the fuse as soon as possible.
PRV 2 open fuse The digital output fuse for PLC 2 (F11) has blown. This will prevent PLC 2 from opening the PRV valves. Replace the fuse as soon as possible.
Hydraulic fluid level low The hydraulic fluid level has dropped below the float level. This alarm may be also be caused by an electrical circuit failure.
PT5 range error The signal received from PT5 is not within the acceptable range. If PT5 is in service when this alarm occurs, PT5 should be disabled until it can be replaced.
PT6 range error The signal received from PT6 is not within the acceptable range. If PT6 is in service when this alarm occurs, PT6 should be disabled until it can be replaced.
PT7 range error The signal received from PT7 is not within the acceptable range. If PT7 is in service when this alarm occurs, PT7 should be disabled until it can be replaced.
PT8 range error The signal received from PT8 is not within the acceptable range. If PT8 is in service when this alarm occurs, PT8 should be disabled until it can be replaced.
PRV#1 is venting Excessive pressure has been detected in PRV 1 and is venting to relieve pressure.
PRV#2 is venting Excessive pressure has been detected in PRV 2 and is venting to relieve pressure.
Hydraulic pressure low Hydraulic pressure is below operating pressure.
PRV1 close pressure low The close pressure for PRV 1 differs from the supplied hydraulic pressure by more than 20 psi. On a hot day, this may indicate fluid expansion. Excess pressure can be released through the bypass valve.
PRV2 close pressure low The close pressure for PRV 2 differs from the supplied hydraulic pressure by more than 20 psi. On a hot day, this may indicate fluid expansion. Excess pressure can be released through the bypass valve.
Victus communications fault Victus™ Operation is not connected to the PRV.
Air pressure low The air pressure has dropped below a reasonable operating pressure.
Set point too high The PRV set point was set above 1600 psi. Pressure will still be relieved at 1600 psi, but the set point should be changed to a reasonable value.
PT5 is disabled PT5 is disabled and will not contribute to the detection of excessive pressure.
PT6 is disabled PT6 is disabled and will not contribute to the detection of excessive pressure.
PT7 is disabled PT7 is disabled and will not contribute to the detection of excessive pressure.
PT8 is disabled PT8 is disabled and will not contribute to the detection of excessive pressure.
Hydraulic over-pressure Hydraulic pressure exceeds 2200 psi. The regulated air pressure must be reduced until the hydraulic pressure is below 2200 psi.
PLC error The PLC has a hardware fault that must be corrected. A qualified technician must investigate and repair the issue.
Sensor Blind - no functioning sensors All of the pressure sensors (PT5 - PT8) are disabled or out of range.
