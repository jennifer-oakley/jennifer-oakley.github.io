About active alarms

The Active page lists the status of all active alarms triggered by sensors failures and exceeded limits. When Victus™ Operation loses communication with one of the sensors connected to the Victus™ MPD System, an alarm will be triggered and the status banner will flash yellow with an audible signal to alert you about the alarm. This also happens when one of the limits on the Setup > Limits and Alarms page is exceeded. An orange warning symbol () will also appear in the banner. See " Limits and Alarms".

There are three alarm statuses on the Active page:

  • Alarm – A new alarm has been triggered by a failed sensor or an exceeded limit. New alarms are marked in red on the Active page.
  • Snoozed – The alarm has been ignored, but the problem that triggered the alarm has not been resolved. Snoozed alarms are marked in yellow. See "Ignoring an alarm".
  • Resolved – The problem that triggered the alarm has been resolved, and can be removed from the Active page. Resolved alarms are marked in green. See "Acknowledging an alarm".
Tip

To snooze or acknowledge all active alarms, click the Snooze/Acknowledge All button ().

Ignoring an alarm

If you want to ignore an alarm on the Active page, you can dismiss the alarm. When you dismiss an alarm, Victus™ Operation will no longer alert you about it. However, you cannot remove the alarm from the Active page until the alarm has been resolved.

To dismiss an alarm:

  1. Go to the Alarms > Active page.
  2. Select the alarm you want to ignore.
  3. Click the Snooze/Acknowledge button ().

The alarm status will change to Snoozed. The status banner will stop flashing, and the audible alert will stop.

Acknowledging an alarm

After an alarm has been resolved, you can acknowledge it to remove it from the Active page.

To acknowledge a resolved alarm:

  1. Go to the Alarms > Active page.
  2. Select the alarm you want to acknowledge.
  3. Click the Snooze/Acknowledge button ().

The alarm will be removed from the Active page, and the alarm status will be updated on the History page.
