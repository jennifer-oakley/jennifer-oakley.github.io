On the Plan screen, you can import the planned survey and compare it to the raw survey data. Planned survey data must contain columns for MD, inclination, and azimuth.

If any of the surveys in the imported data are in the no-go zone, the surveys in the no-go zone will be highlighted in red. Any survey that exceeds the inclination or azimuth limits is considered to be in the no-go zone.

  • The default inclination limit for all survey types is 70 degrees.
  • For non-IFR surveys, the default azimuth limit is ± 30 degrees from east or west.
  • For IFR surveys, the default azimuth limit is ± 25 degrees from east or west.

To change the inclination and azimuth limits, go to the Settings menu, then go to the No Go Zone section in the Settings Editor window. See "No-go zone settings".

Importing plan data

To import plan data:

  1. Go to the Plan screen.
  2. Click the Load Plan Data button on the right side of the screen.
  3. In the window that opens, navigate to the file you want to import and click Open.
  4. Enter the tie-on point in the Survey Tie Point section at the bottom of the screen.

The imported data will appear in the panel on the right side of the screen. If any of the surveys in the imported data are in the no-go zone, the surveys in the no-go zone will be highlighted in red. The planned survey will be graphed in the Top View and Section View charts on the left side of the screen.

Survey charts

The charts on the left side of the screen show the top view and section view of the survey data. You can use these charts to compare the planned vs. actual surveys.

  • The blue line shows the planned survey data. See "Importing plan data".
  • The red line shows the raw survey data, imported on the Inputs screen. See "Raw Survey Data".

To maximize the graphs, click the Maximize button () at the top of the panel.

Field definitions

The table below defines the columns in the Plan Data panel.

Column definitions for the Plan Data panel
Field Name Definition
MD Measured depth of the planned survey point
Inc Inclination at the survey point's MD
Az True (R) Azimuth at the survey point's MD
TVD True vertical depth at the survey point's MD
N Offset

North offset of the Planned survey from the site or slot center in the local North direction
E Offset

East offset of the Planned survey from the site or slot center in the local East direction
DLS Dogleg severity of the well path between this point and the previous salient point
Toolface Angle of the toolface from the highside of the wellbore
Build Rate Change in inclination of the wellpath between this point and the previous survey point
Turn Rate Effective change in azimuth of the between this point and the previous survey point
CL Change in measured depth between this point and the previous point
VS Projected distance of the survey point from the vertical section starting position, along the vertical section azimuth

The table below defines the fields in the Survey Tie Point section.

Field definitions for the Survey Tie Point section
Field Name Definition
MD Measured depth of the tie-on point from the drill floor
Inc Inclination of well at the tie-on point, with zero degrees being vertical down
Az Azimuth of well at the tie-on point, measured clockwise from the site North reference
North Offset North offset of the tie-on point from the site or slot center in the local North direction
East Offset East offset of the tie-on point from the site or slot center in the local East direction
TVD True vertical depth of the tie-on point, measured from the TVD reference point
