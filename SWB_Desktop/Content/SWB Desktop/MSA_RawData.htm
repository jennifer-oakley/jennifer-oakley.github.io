  • Raw Survey Data

The MSA > Raw Data tab is used to assess the quality of the raw survey data and exclude data that exceeds the tolerance levels. The table on the Raw Data tab is color-coded to indicate the results of the quality control assessment.

  • Green surveys fall within the tolerance levels for all QC parameters. By default, all green surveys are used for MSA optimization.
  • Red surveys exceed the tolerance level for at least one QC parameter. All red surveys are excluded from MSA optimization.
  • All remaining surveys are shown in orange. Surveys may be shown in amber due to external magnetic interference or expected effects of the BHA magnetization at that attitude. By default, all orange surveys are excluded from MSA optimization. However, you should examine orange surveys to determine if they should be included. If in doubt, it is typically best to exclude the data. See "MSA Optimization".
Note

The majority of the raw survey data should be color-coded in green or orange. If most or all of the raw survey data is color-coded red, the reference values entered on the Inputs tab may be incorrect. See "Magnetic Parameters".

Note

The majority of the raw survey data should be color-coded in green or orange. If most or all of the raw survey data is color-coded red, the reference values entered on the Inputs tab may be incorrect. See "Magnetic Parameters". If most or all of the raw surveys do not have an FMS observation, the reference values entered in the FMS Files window may be incorrect. See "FMS Data".

The table below defines the tolerance levels used to assess the raw survey data.

Tolerance levels for raw survey data
Parameter Passing Tolerance Failing Tolerance
Total Field Strength (BTOTAL) ±350 nT ±1050 nT
Dip (DIP) ±0.3° ±0.9°
Total Gravity (GTOTAL) ±0.003 g ±0.009 g

After the data has been assessed, you can also view graphs of the QC results in the lower left panel. These graphs show the changes in magnetic field strength, dip, and gravitation field strength. To maximize the graphs, click the Maximize button () at the top of the panel.

The leftmost button at the top of the screen shows the status of the raw survey data file. If there haven't been any changes to the file, the Up To Date button () will be shown. When the file is updated, the Refresh button () will be shown instead. If the Beep option is selected, Surveyor's Workbench will also beep continuously until the file changes are loaded into Surveyor's Workbench. To load the new data into Surveyor's Workbench, click the Refresh button (). The quality of the data will be automatically re-assesed on the MSA > Raw Data tab, and any FMS data will be automatically synced to the new survey data. See "FMS Data".
