About wellpath planning

Wellpath planning in 5D gives you everything you need to design, edit, and manage well trajectories. Whether you're creating your first well plan, making changes while drilling, or juggling multiple plan revisions, 5D has the flexibility and precision to get the job done.

5D tracks all of your plan versions automatically, so you can create multiple designs, compare them, and go back to earlier versions whenever drilling conditions change. Plans are built using salient points. Salient points are the points on the wellpath that mark a change in curvature e.g. from a straight to a curved section. You connect these points with different types of well segments to a create smooth, drillable wellpath that actually works in the real world.-

Types of wells

5D handles three different types of wells:

  • Main wells kick off from your drill floor. These are your standard wellbores that start at surface and end at the target.
  • Sidetracks branch off from an existing well at whatever depth you choose. The starting point and direction are locked in by where you are on the parent well.
  • Tie-in wells also start from a parent well, but in this case the parent well isn't in your current database. You just start with known coordinates and attitude.

Well plan organization

5D keeps your well plans organized in a way that makes sense:

  • Your current plan is the one you're working on right now. It's the only plan you can actually edit and change. All your planning work happens here.
  • The plan archive is where 5D stores your saved plan versions. Plans are organized into folders that usually represent major changes or different phases of your well.

Planning workflow

Here's how most people work with wellpath planning in 5D:

  1. Start with your basic design by dropping in anchor points and connecting them with curves. You can build your wellpath by tacking sections onto the end, or by aiming for target sets you've already defined.
  2. Fine-tune everything by editing your anchor points, adding new control points, or moving whole sections around.
  3. Keep your plans organized by saving revisions, creating reactive plans when real drilling doesn't match your original design, and managing archived versions.
  4. Use the specialized tools when you need them - things like Auto-KOP for automatic kick-off calculations, or the "Two of Seven" method when you're dealing with complex multi-section designs.

Key capabilities

5D's wellpath planning gives you some pretty powerful tools:

  • Build wellpaths however you need using straight sections, curves, curve-hold segments, and complex composite curves. Pretty much any trajectory you can think of, you can build.
  • Flexible anchor points that adapt intelligently as your wellpath changes. You can tell a point to keep its position fixed while everything else adjusts, or lock in the curve parameters while the position moves. 5D figures out the rest.
  • Reactive planning lets you deal with reality. When your actual surveys don't match the plan, you can combine real data with your planned trajectory to create updated plans that make sense.
  • Target integration means you can aim your wellpath at target sets and let 5D figure out how to get there. It'll thread through multiple targets automatically while still giving you control over the details.
  • Advanced editing features for moving whole sections around, rotating parts of your wellpath, or sliding sections along their current direction. You can make precise adjustments without rebuilding everything from scratch.

All these features work together whether you're planning a simple vertical well or wrestling with a complex multi-lateral design that has to thread through tight geological constraints.
