About planning methods

Planning methods in 5D give you different ways to create wellpath segments between salient points. Whether you need a simple straight section or a complex multi-curve trajectory, understanding these methods helps you build wellpaths that meet your operational requirements.

5D offers two main categories of planning methods: single section methods for straightforward connections, and composite section methods for more complex trajectories. Each method gives you different ways to control the geometry, so you can specify the parameters that matter most for your particular situation.

The beauty of 5D's planning methods is their flexibility. You might know exactly where you want to end up but not care about the path, or you might need precise control over curvature and attitude. The different methods let you specify what you know and let 5D calculate the rest.

These planning methods appear as different tabs when you're adding, appending, or inserting anchor points in your wellpath.

Single section methods

Single section methods create one wellpath segment between two points:

  • Straight section – Extends the wellpath in a straight line maintaining the same attitude. Control the endpoint by specifying measured depth or true vertical depth.
  • Curve to position – Creates a constant-radius curve to reach a specific target location. Specify the target coordinates and 5D calculates the attitude, length, and dogleg severity needed.
  • Curve to attitude - Creates a curve that ends with a specific inclination and azimuth. You can create the curve using either a fixed length or a fixed dogleg severity.
  • Curve with curvature – Creates a curve with specified dogleg severity, tool-face angle, and length. 5D calculates the final position and attitude based on your drilling constraints.

Composite section methods

Composite methods create multiple wellpath segments in one operation:

  • CurveHold - Maximizes straight drilling time by using minimum curvature to reach the target, then drilling straight. Starts with a curved section followed by a straight section.
  • CurveHoldCurve – Connects two fully defined points (position and attitude) using two curved sections separated by a straight tangent section. Most robust method for complex connections.
  • DoubleCurve – Connects fully defined points using only two curved sections with no straight section between them. Less robust than curveHoldCurve but useful in specific situations.
