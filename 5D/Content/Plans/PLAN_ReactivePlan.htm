About reactive well planning

Reactive well planning helps you adapt when the actual drilled wellpath diverges from your original plan. Instead of forcing the wellpath back to the original plan (which can create tortuous geometry), reactive planning creates a new plan that starts from your current survey position and reconnects to the original plan at a more practical point.

This approach is particularly useful when drilling conditions don't match expectations - for example, when your bottom hole assembly isn't performing as planned or geological conditions require trajectory adjustments. Rather than creating complex corrections that increase drilling time and costs, you can develop a smoother path forward.

Reactive well planning offers several advantages over trying to force the wellpath back to the original plan:

  • Reduces wellpath tortuosity by avoiding severe trajectory corrections.
  • Minimizes drilling complexity and associated time delays.
  • Avoids unnecessary equipment changes like switching to higher build rate assemblies.
  • Maintains target objectives while working with current drilling conditions.
  • Reduces operational costs by eliminating premature trips and bit changes.

5D automatically archives your current plan before creating the reactive plan, so you maintain a complete record of planning decisions throughout the drilling process.

Creating reactive plans

A reactive plan starts from the end of your current definitive survey and reconnects to your existing plan at a specified point. You can control how the connection is made and how much of the original plan to preserve.

To create a reactive plan:

  1. Right-click the current plan in the Data List pane and select New Reactive Plan, or click the New Reactive Plan button () in the data table.
  2. In the New Reactive Plan window, review the final definitive survey point at the top - this is where your new plan will start
  3. Select how to handle the geometry beyond the connection point, using the options below the final definitive survey point.
    • Preserve Position - Maintain exact positions and attitudes of remaining anchor points
    • Preserve Original Curve Specifications - Recreate sections using original design parameters
    • Preserve Shape - Keep the same wellpath shape beyond the tie point
  4. Choose how to reconnect using the Connect by options.
  5. Enter the dogleg severities (DLS1 and DLS2) for the connecting composite curve
  6. Click OK to create the plan

The reactive plan will be created by joining your definitive survey to the selected reconnection point using composite curves (curveHoldCurve, doubleCurve, or holdDoubleCurve) with your specified dogleg severities.

Note

If your current plan differs from the original plan, an archive copy will be automatically created before implementing the reactive plan. You can delete this plan if it's not needed.

Field definitions

The table below defines the fields in the New Reactive Plan window.

Fields in the New Reactive Plan window
Field Name Description
Final Definitive Survey Point

Display showing the last point in your definitive survey - this becomes the starting point for the reactive plan. Cannot be edited.
Rejoin Options

Method for handling wellpath geometry beyond the connection point

  • Preserve Position – Maintains exact positions and attitudes of all anchor points beyond the connection
  • Preserve Original Curve Specifications – Recreates all sections beyond the connection using their original design parameters
  • Preserve Shape – Maintains the same wellpath shape beyond the tie point as the original plan
Connect By

Method for joining the definitive survey to the current plan

  • Anchor Point – Connects to a specific anchor or ordinary point (ordinary points are automatically promoted)
  • MD – Connects to an interpolated point at the specified measured depth
  • TVD – Connects to an interpolated point at the specified true vertical depth
  • None – Creates a plan containing only the definitive survey
Rejoin to Anchor Point

Method for connecting the definitive survey to the current plan.

  • Curve Hold Curve – Creates a curve-hold-curve segment between the definitive survey and the current plan.
  • Double Curve – Creates a double curve segment between the definitive survey and the current plan.
  • Hold Double Curve – Creates a hold-double-curve segment between the definitive survey and the current plan.
DLS1

Preferred dogleg severity of the shallow curved section.
DLS2

Preferred dogleg severity of the deep curved section.
DLS Iteration Method

Method for preserving dogleg severity values during iteration.

  • Preserve DLS1/DLS2 Diff – Preserves the difference between first and second dogleg severity.
  • Preserve DLS1 – Preserves the first dogleg severity.
  • Preserve DLS2 – Preserves the second dogleg severity.
Apply after every increment

Automatically applies changes after each parameter modification
