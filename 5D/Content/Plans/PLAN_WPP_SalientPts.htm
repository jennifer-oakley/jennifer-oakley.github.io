About salient points

Every wellpath in 5D is made up of connected sections that form a continuous trajectory from surface to target. While the wellpath appears as one smooth curve, it's actually built from a series of curved and straight segments that connect at specific locations called salient points.

Salient points are the fundamental elements that define your wellpath geometry. Each point contains enough information to describe the wellpath at that location: its position in 3D space, the direction it's heading, and what type of point it is.

  • Position tells you exactly where the point sits in 3D space. This includes the true vertical depth (TVD), northing, and easting coordinates based on your projected coordinate system and vertical reference datum.
  • Attitude describes which direction the wellpath is heading at that point. This is defined by the inclination (how far from vertical) and azimuth (which compass direction).
  • Type indicates what kind of salient point you're dealing with, which affects how 5D handles it during editing operations.

Salient point types

There are three types of salient points:

  • Survey points represent actual measured survey data and appear as red spheres in the 3D Viewer. These points represent reality – what's actually been drilled based on survey measurements taken during drilling operations.
  • Anchor points are the control points you use to design your wellpath, shown as green spheres. These are the points whose locations and attitudes you want to maintain when you're editing the wellpath.
  • Ordinary points are intermediate points that 5D creates automatically when building complex curve segments. They appear as white spheres and are basically helper points that connect your anchor points.

Well curve segments

When you connects two adjacent points, 5D assumes the curvature is constant and uses the minimum curvature method to determine the wellpath. 5D has four types of composite well curves for creating complex, drillable trajectories that meet operational constraints.

  • Curve segments are basic curve segments that connect two salient points with a constant radius curve. Curve segments can be defined based on the position, attitude along the wellpath, or curvature along the wellpath.
  • CurveHold segments contain a curve section, followed by a straight section. When you provide the attitude and position of the start point with the position of the end point, 5D will calculate the attitude and position of the intermediate point, where the segment transitions from curved to straight.
  • CurveHoldCurve segments connect two points that are fully defined in both position and attitude. CurveHoldCurve segments connect these points using a curveHold segment, followed by another curve segment.
  • DoubleCurve segments also connect two points that are fully defined in both position and attitude. DoubleCurve segments connect these points using two curve sections. Unlike a curveHoldCurve segment, doubleCurve segments do not contain a straight section. DoubleCurve segments are defined based on the curvature of the first curve section. If you specify the curvature of both curve sections, 5D may need to add a straight section at the beginning of the segment to make the geometry work.
