About anti-collision analysis

Anti-collision analysis predicts the possibility of a collision between wells. There may be a risk of collision when planning a well in close proximity to an existing well, or when planning multiple wells in close proximity to one another. If anti-collision analysis determines that there is a significant risk of collision, you can update the current plan to reduce the collision risk. If the collision risk is with an existing well, you may also want to consider re-surveying the existing well with a higher accuracy tool.

For each planned and drilled wellpath included in the analysis, anti-collision analysis calculates the uncertainty envelope around the wellpath. Survey readings can estimate the location of the wellpath at regular intervals, but the accuracy of the readings is limited by the accuracy of the survey tool. The uncertainty envelope represents the amount of uncertainty of the precise location of the wellpath.

5D uses the ISCWSA Industry Steering Committee on Wellbore Survey Accuracy method to calculate an ellipsoid of uncertainty around each survey station, which is used to calculate an enclosing, two-dimensional ellipse that is perpendicular to the direction of the wellpath at each survey station. These ellipses are interpolated to calculate an uncertainty envelope that surrounds the wellpath. See "Uncertainty Ellipsoids".

Anti-collision analysis calculates an uncertainty envelope for the primary wellpath and all nearby wellpaths. It also calculates a series of center-to-center lines that connect the wellpaths at regular intervals. Based on the center-to-center lines (Cr-Cr) and the radius of the uncertainty envelopes, a separation factor is calculated to determine the risk of collision. A high separation factor indicates a greater space between the uncertainty envelopes, and therefore, a lower risk of collision. A low separation factor indicates a smaller space between the uncertainty envelopes and a higher risk of collision.

There are three basic steps for running an anti-collision analysis:

  1. Update the analysis settings. The analysis parameters allow you to chose the calculation methods for the uncertainty envelopes and separation factors. See "Anti-collision options".
  2. Select the primary wellpath. The primary well is the well you are analyzing. Separation factors and center-to-center lines are calculated in relation to the primary wellpath. See "Configuring anti-collision analysis".
  3. Select the secondary wellpaths. Secondary wellpaths are any nearby wells that may pose a risk of collision. See "Configuring anti-collision analysis".

Before you can run an anti-collision analysis, anti-collision must be turned on. If anti-collision is not turned on, click the Turn Anti-Collision On/Off button () in the main toolbar.

Using the Anti-Collision window

The Anti-Collision window, located in the Data Entry pane at the bottom of the screen, allows you to view calculation results, change the primary well, and create a report for the analysis results. To open the Anti-Collision window, click the Anti-Collision Data Window button () in the main toolbar.

Buttons in the Anti-Collision window
Button Function
AC Warnings Displays any warnings encountered during the analysis
Change Primary Opens the Select Primary Well Path window, which allows you to change the primary well. See "Configuring anti-collision analysis".
Report Generates a report for the analysis results. See "Anti-collision reports".
> Closes the settings panel on the right side of the Anti-Collision window
< Opens the settings panel, which contains analysis settings and warnings. See "Anti-collision options".
