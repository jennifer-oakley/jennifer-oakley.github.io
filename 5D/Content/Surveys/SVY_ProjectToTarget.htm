About projecting to targets

The project to target feature helps you assess whether you can reach geological targets from your current drilling position. This analysis is useful during drilling operations when you need to evaluate trajectory options or determine if course corrections are needed to hit your planned targets.

When you project a survey to a target, 5D creates multiple curved wellpath projections from the last survey point (or from the projected bit location if you've projected the survey to the bit) to various points around your target volume. This gives you a range of trajectory options to evaluate.

Projecting a survey to a target

To project a survey to a target:

  1. In the Data List pane, right-click on the definitive survey and select the Project to Target option.
  2. In the Project To Target window that opens, go to the Target Set field and select the target set that contains the target you want to project to.
  3. In the Target field, select the desired target.

The projection analysis runs automatically and displays the results in both a graphical view and a data table. The analysis creates curved wellpaths from your survey endpoint to regularly spaced points around the target volume.

Analyzing projection results

The projection results are displayed in two formats: a three-dimensional view and a parameters table.

The 3D view shows your target volume with multiple curved wellpaths connecting from the survey endpoint to different points around the target. For cuboid targets, curves connect to each corner and the center (nine total curves). The number of curves varies based on the target shape you've defined.

The Project To Target window shows detailed trajectory information for each projected curve, including:

  • Maximum inclination and azimuth changes required
  • Curve length and total measured depth
  • Build and turn rates needed
  • Target point coordinates

You can use this data to evaluate which trajectory options are most feasible based on your drilling capabilities and equipment limitations.
