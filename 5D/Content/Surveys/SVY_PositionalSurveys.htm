About positional surveys

Positional surveys work differently from normal surveys. Instead of using measured depth (MD), inclination, and azimuth to calculate position coordinates, positional surveys use known TVD, north offset, and east offset coordinates to calculate the MD, inclination, and azimuth values. This approach is useful when you have precise position data (such as from gyroscopic surveys or other high-accuracy positioning systems) and need to derive the directional drilling parameters.

Survey point data is shown in the Positional Survey Editor window. The first point is the tie-on point, which can't be edited through the editor. When there are adjacent positional surveys that need to be joined, 5D creates intermediate points automatically. These points appear in the editor but can't be selected or edited using the right-click options. You can distinguish between regular survey points and intermediate points because the regular positional survey points have checkboxes next to them in the list.

Creating a positional survey

To create a positional survey:

  1. In the Data List pane, right-click on the well where you want to add the survey and select Add Survey.
  2. In the Survey Editor - New window, click the Survey Options button to open the Survey Options window.
  3. In the Survey Type field, select the Positional SAG D option.
  4. Click OK to close the Survey Options window.
  5. Fill out the remaining survey information and enter the tie-on point. See "Survey properties".
  6. Click Import to add survey points. See "Entering survey points manually" and "Pasting survey data from the clipboard".
  7. To add the survey to the definitive survey, select the Add New Survey Points to Definitive Survey option.
  8. Click the OK button to close the window and create the survey.

The new survey will be listed in the Data List pane, under the survey archive on the List Surveys tab. To edit the survey, export the survey to a file, or create a survey report, go to the Data List pane and right-click on the survey name. See "Surveys".

Entering survey points manually

To add survey points manually:

  1. Right-click on an existing point in the list.
  2. Select Insert Before or Insert After depending on where you want to add the new point.
  3. In the Positional Survey Element window that opens, enter the TVD, north offset, and east offset coordinates.
  4. Click OK to add the point.

The new survey point will be added to the list and the MD, inclination, and azimuth values will be calculated automatically. Any intermediate points required to join the point to the survey will be automatically created.

Pasting survey data from the clipboard

Copied survey data must contain three columns: TVD, north offset, and east offset. The data must contain values only and no headers. To adjust the column mapping for your data, go to the File menu in the Positional Survey Editor window and select the Clipboard Column Indices option.

To paste survey data from the clipboard:

  1. Copy your survey data to the clipboard. The data must contain three columns with TVD, north offset, and east offset values (values only, no headers).
  2. Right-click on an existing point in the survey list.
  3. Select the Paste option.

The new survey points will be added to the list and all calculated values (MD, inclination, azimuth) will be generated automatically for each point. Any intermediate points required to join the point to the survey will be automatically created.

Adding to the definitive survey

When you combine positional surveys with normal surveys in the definitive survey, you may notice a discontinuity (sharp kink) at the junction between the two survey types. This is an artifact of the different calculation methods used by each survey type.

To smooth out these discontinuities, you can apply a survey correction:

  1. In the Data List pane, right-click on the definitive survey that contains both normal and positional survey data.
  2. Select the Survey Correction option.

The survey correction will be applied automatically and requires no additional input. However, you must reapply the correction manually each time you update the definitive survey.
