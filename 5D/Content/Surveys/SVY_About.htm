About surveys

When drilling begins, survey readings are taken at regular intervals to determine the actual position of the well. Surveys are at the bottom of the hierarchy in a 5D database. On the List Surveys tab in the Data List pane, you can view the definitive survey and the survey archive for the selected well. In the Data List pane, a survey is represented by the Survey icon (). The definitive survey is a composite survey of each individual survey for the well, and the survey archive stores all of well's surveys.

Definitive survey

To update the definitive survey, right-click on the definitive survey in the Data List pane or go to Plans / Surveys > Definitive Survey in the main menu.

  • Edit/View Definitive Survey – Opens the Survey Editor - Edit window, which allows you to edit the properties of the definitive survey. See "Survey properties".
  • Edit Drill Floor – Opens the Edit Drill Floor window, which allows you change the elevation of the drill floor for the definitive survey. See "Drill floor elevation".
  • Clear Survey – Deletes all survey points in the definitive survey. This action does not affect the individual surveys that have been added to the well.
  • Definitive Survey Summary – Opens the Definitive Survey Summary window, which lists all of the individual surveys included in the definitive survey. See "Definitive Survey".
  • Anti-Collision Primary – Sets the definitive survey for the selected well as the primary wellpath in an anti-collision analysis. See "Anti-Collision Analysis".
  • Project to Target – Opens the Project to Target window, which provides several possible wellpaths to join the definitive survey to a target. See "Projecting Surveys to Targets".
  • Proximity Analysis – Opens the Lease Line Proximity window, which calculates the horizontal distance between the definitive survey and the lease lines associated with the well. See "Lease Line Proximity Analysis".
  • Report – Generates a report for the definitive survey or uncertainty. See "Survey reports".
  • Plot – Opens the Plot window, which allows you to plot the definitive survey or traveling cylinder. See "Plotting".
  • Export to File – Exports the definitive survey to an MMS, LAS, REP, or CSV file. See "Exporting surveys to a file".
  • Interpolate – Interpolates the properties of a point between salient points in the definitive survey. See "Survey interpolation".
  • Export Wellpath – Exports the definitive survey to a text file.
  • Edit Survey Programme – Opens the Edit Survey Programme window, which allows you to set the survey tools used at different depth ranges along the wellpath. These settings can be used to override the survey program for the definitive survey in an anti-collision analysis. See "Anticipated survey program".

Survey archive

To update the survey archive, right-click on the survey archive in the Data List pane or go to Plans / Surveys > Survey Archive in the main menu.

  • Add Survey – Adds a new survey to the well. See "New surveys".
  • Delete Surveys Recursively – Deletes all surveys from the well. This action cannot be undone.

Surveys

After you create a survey, you can edit the survey properties, export the wellpath, create reports, and more. To update a survey, right-click on the survey name in the Data List pane or go to Plans / Surveys > Surveys in the main menu.

  • Edit Survey – Opens the Survey Editor - Edit window, which allows you to edit the properties of the survey. See "Survey properties".
  • Report – Generates a report for the selected survey. See "Survey reports".
  • Plot – Opens the Plot window, which allows you to plot the selected survey. See "Plotting".
  • Export to File – Exports the selected survey to an MMS, LAS, REP, or CSV file. See "Exporting surveys to a file".
  • Interpolate – Interpolates the properties of a point between salient points in the selected survey. See "Survey interpolation".
  • Export Wellpath – Exports the selected survey to a text file.
  • Delete Survey – Deletes the survey from the well. If the survey points have been added to the definitive survey, they will not be deleted from the definitive survey.
