About the definitive survey

The definitive survey is a composite survey of each individual survey for the well, and it can be used for anti-collision analysis and reactive well planning. See "Anti-Collision Analysis" and See "Reactive well planning". The definitive surveys of main wells are referenced to the drill floor, and the definitive surveys of tie-on wells are referenced to the tie-on point for the parent well.

To view the definitive survey for a well, go to the List Surveys tab in the Data List pane. When you select the definitive survey, the survey points will appear in the Data Table pane. If the Data Table pane is not visible, click the Data Table Window button () in the main toolbar.

The Definitive Survey Summary window shows the individual surveys that are included in the definitive survey. To open the Definitive Survey Summary window, right-click on the definitive survey and select the Definitive Survey Summary option.

Adding points to the definitive survey

To add survey points from an individual survey to the definitive survey:

  1. Go to the List Surveys tab in the Data List pane.
  2. Navigate to the survey containing the survey points you want to add to the definitive survey.
  3. In the data table, click on the starting survey point that you want to add.
  4. Press the Shift key, and click on the ending survey point that you want to add.
  5. Right-click on the data table and select the Add to Def. Survey option.

The selected survey points will be added to the definitive survey. Any existing survey points that fall within the same depth range will be replaced with the new survey points.

Projecting the survey to the bit

To project the definitive survey to the bit:

  1. Go to Tools > Options and Settings in the main menu.
  2. In the Options and Settings window, select the Wellpath page.
  3. In the Definitive Survey section, select the Project sensor reading to bit option.
  4. In the Sensor layback from bit field, enter the distance from the last survey point to the bit.
  5. Click OK to close the window.

An extrapolated point for the bit will be added to the definitive surveys for all wells in the 5D database. The bit position will be calculated based on the current trend for toolface and dogleg severity.
