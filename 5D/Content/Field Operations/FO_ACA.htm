Proximity analysis

The Proximity Analysis table shows the proximity of the last survey point to the selected secondary wellpaths. These results include actual survey points and projected survey points. By default, the Proximity Analysis table is sorted by separation factor. To sort by a different parameter, click on the column heading.

The CC, ES, SF, and AC101 WL columns are color-coded to indicate the risk of collision.

  • Red indicates there is a high risk of collision.
  • Yellow or orange indicates there is a medium risk of collision.
  • Green indicates there is a low risk of collision.

To update the risk thresholds or analysis settings for anti-collision analysis, go to Tools > Options and Settings > Anti-Collision Parameters in the main menu. See "Anti-collision parameters".

Warning

If the separation factor for a wellpath is less than 2.5, it is strongly advised to create a tracking sheet to track proximity. See "AC 101 tracking". In addition, the recovery section DLS should not exceed 1°/30 m. See "Survey settings".

The table below defines the columns in the Proximity Analysis table.

Column definitions for the Proximity Analysis table
Column Name Definition
Nearest Well

Name of the nearest well, based on the current sort method.

  • (p) indicates the current plan was used in the analysis.
  • (s) indicates the definitive survey was used in the analysis.
CC Center-to-center distance between the primary wellpath and the secondary wellpath with the smallest separation factor
ES Distance between the ellipsoids of uncertainty along the center-to-center vector
SF Separation factor between the wellpath and the closest well
Pri MD Measured depth of the point on the primary wellpath

AC 101 tracking

AC101 tracking sheets continuously track the proximity of survey points and projected points to each secondary wellpath. For each secondary wellpath, the tracking sheet lists all survey points and projected points entered in the Field Operations window. It also shows alerts and recommended actions for each point, based on the proximity of the secondary wellpath to the primary wellpath.

When new survey points are entered in the Field Operations window, you can regenerate the tracking sheet to see the updated proximity results for the new survey points.

To create an AC101 tracking sheet:

  1. Click the Settings button () in the toolbar at the top of the Field Operations window, then go to the Survey Settings tab and configure the projection distances for projecting to the bit and projecting beyond the bit. See "Configuring survey projection".
  2. Go to the Survey Projection panel and select the projection methods for projecting to the bit and projecting beyond the bit. See "Projecting beyond the survey".
  3. Add additional projections as needed. See "Adding projections".
  4. Click the arrow next to the Excel Report Options button () and select the AC101 option.
  5. When new survey data is received, double-click inside the blank red row and enter the data for the new survey point. See "Adding survey points".
  6. Regenerate the tracking sheet as needed.

The generated report will include the separation factors and center-to-center distances for each survey point added in the Field Operations window, as well as for the projected points.

Note

You must close the tracking sheet before you can generate a new version.
