About changing parent slots

Changing the parent slot allows you to move a main well or sidetrack from one slot to another slot in the field. Once you move the well, it becomes a main well that starts from the new slot.

When you change a well's parent slot, you can choose how to connect the well to its new location and which portions of the original wellpath to keep. The system builds a new wellpath that keeps the important parts of your original design while connecting it to the new slot.

Note

You cannot change the parent slot of a well that has surveys associated with it. Wells must be in the planning stage without any actual survey data.

Changing a well's parent slot

To move a well to a different slot:

  1. In the Data List pane, right-click the well you want to move.
  2. Select Change Well Hierarchy > Change Parent Slot. Alternatively, you can go to Field Items > Well > Change Well Hierarchy > Change Parent Slot in the main menu.
  3. In the Select Slot window, choose the new slot for the well and click OK.
  4. In the Change Parent Slot window that appears, configure the connection options:
    • In the Choose Tie Point section, enter the depth of the tie-on point and select whether you're specifying MD or TVD.
    • In the Choose re-connection method to original plan section, select how you want to connect the original plan to the tie-on point.
    • If you selected Preserve well path shape and position beyond specified point, specify the connection point in the Specify Connection Point section.
    • Configure the curve parameters in the Join Tie Point to Connection Point section.
  5. Click Apply to preview the changes, or click OK to apply the changes and close the window.

The well will be moved to the new slot with a wellpath designed according to your specifications. The necessary curve sections and intermediates will be calculated to move the original wellpath to the new slot.

Field definitions

The table below defines the fields and options in the Change Parent Slot window.

Fields in the Change Parent Slot window
Field Description
Choose Tie Point

Depth of the tie-on point where the new wellpath will connect to the original plan.

  • Depth – MD or TVD of the tie-on point.
  • MD (rel to parent df) – Indicates that the entered depth is the MD of the tie-on point.
  • TVD (rel to parent df) – Indicates that the entered depth is the TVD of the tie-on point.
Choose reconnection method to original plan

Method for connecting the tie-point to the original plan.

  • No connection – Does not connect to the original plan. The new wellpath contains only the path from the new slot to the tie point.
  • Preserve well planning parameters – Ensures all sections beyond the tie point are recreated according to their original design specifications.
  • Preserve well path shape – Ensures the wellpath shape beyond the tie point remains the same as the original design.
  • Preserve well path shape and position beyond specified point – Ensures all points beyond the specified connection point maintain their position and attitude. This option enables the Specify Connection Point section.
Specify connection point

Depth of the connection point where the new wellpath will connect to the original plan. Only available when the Preserve well path shape and position beyond specified point option is selected.

  • Depth – MD or TVD of the connection point.
  • MD (rel to parent df) – Indicates that the entered depth is the MD of the connection point.
  • TVD (rel to parent df) – Indicates that the entered depth is the TVD of the connection point.
Join Tie Point to Connection Point

Method for connecting the tie-on point to the connection point.

  • Curve Hold Curve – Creates a curve-hold-curve segment between the tie-on point and the connection point.
  • Double Curve – Creates a double curve segment between the tie-on point and the connection point.
  • Hold Double Curve – Creates a hold-double-curve segment between the tie-on point and the connection point.
DLS1

Preferred dogleg severity of the shallow curved section.
DLS2

Preferred dogleg severity of the deep curved section.
DLS Iteration Method

Method for preserving dogleg severity values during iteration.

  • Preserve DLS1/DLS2 Diff – Preserves the difference between first and second dogleg severity.
  • Preserve DLS1 – Preserves the first dogleg severity.
  • Preserve DLS2 – Preserves the second dogleg severity.
Translate Plan Archive When checked, translates the plan archive along with the current plan.
Translate Targets When checked, translates any targets associated with the wellpath to the new slot location.
Apply after every increment When checked, automatically applies changes after each parameter edit.
