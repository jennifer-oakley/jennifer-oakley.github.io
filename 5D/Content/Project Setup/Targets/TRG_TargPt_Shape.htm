About target shapes

The target shape is the uncertainly area around the target point. The target shape defines the allowable margin of error for reaching the target point. There are six types of target shapes:

  • Point – There is no uncertainty area around the target point.
  • Cuboid – The uncertainty area is a cube with six rectangular faces. See "Properties of cuboid shapes".
  • Cylinder – The uncertainty area is a cylinder with circular top and bottom faces. See "Properties of a cylinder shape".
  • Elliptical Cylinder – The uncertainty area is a cylinder with elliptical top and bottom faces. See "Properties of an elliptical cylinder".
  • Ellipsoid – The uncertainty area is a sphere. See "Properties of an ellipsoid".
  • Polyhedron – The uncertainty area is composed of three or more connecting polygons. See "Properties of a polyhedron".

The dimensions of each shape are parallel to the north, east, and TVD axes. The orientation of the shape around the target point is determined by the rotation angle.

Volume Removal Style

Method for removing volume from the cylinder.

  • None – Does not remove any volume from the cylinder
  • Segment – Removes a section of volume along the length of a chord.
  • Wedge – Removes a wedge from the center to the start azimuth, and from the center to the end azimuth.
Chord on Left Side

Indicates the end point of the chord is behind the start point, in a clockwise direction. Otherwise, the end point is in front of the start point. Only available when the Segment option is selected.
Start Azimuth

Starting point of the chord or wedge on the circumference of the cylinder, measured clockwise from north.
End Azimuth

End point of the wedge on the circumference of the cylinder, measured clockwise from north. Only available when the Wedge option is selected.
Chord Length

Distance between the start and end points of the chord. Only available when the Segment option is selected.
