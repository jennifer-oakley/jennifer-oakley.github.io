About lease line associations

To perform proximity analysis between wells and lease lines, you must first associate the relevant lease lines with each well. Associating a lease line with a well creates a link between specific lease lines and the wells that fall within their boundaries. This allows you to to analyze how close wellpaths come to regulatory boundaries.

Lease line associations create relationships between wells and the lease lines that may affect their drilling paths. These associations are essential for:

  • Running proximity analysis to ensure regulatory compliance
  • Monitoring wellpath distances from lease boundaries
  • Generating reports that show potential boundary violations
  • Planning wellpaths that maintain safe distances from lease lines

You can associate multiple lease lines with a single well, and each lease line can be associated with multiple wells as needed. The associations don't modify the lease lines themselves – they only create the relationships needed for analysis.

Associating lease lines with wells

To associate lease lines with a well:

  1. Right-click on the well in the Data List pane, then go to the Lease Lines menu and select the Associate option.
  2. In the Associate Lease Lines window that appears, go to the table on the left and select the lease lines you want to associate with the well.
  3. Click the right-facing arrow to copy the lease line to the table on the right.
  4. Repeat for any additional lease lines you want to associate.
  5. Click OK to save the associations.

The well is now linked to the selected lease lines and ready for proximity analysis.

Removing lease line associations

To remove a lease line associations from a well:

  1. Right-click on the well in the Data List pane, then go to the Lease Lines menu and select the Associate option.
  2. In the Associate Lease Lines window that appears, go to the table on the right and select the lease lines you want to remove.
  3. Click the left-facing arrow to remove the lease line from the table on the right.
  4. Click OK to save the associations.

The lease line will be removed from the list of associated lease lines.
