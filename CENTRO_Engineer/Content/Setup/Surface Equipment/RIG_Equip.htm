About equipment configuration

The Setup > Surface Equipment > Equipment Configuration page allows you to select the equipment on the rig, as well as the size and type of rig. The rig equipment available on the Equipment Configuration page includes blowout preventers, auxiliary pumps, and RCDs. See "Selecting rig equipment".

Video: Entering rig information

This video tutorial will teach you how to enter the basic information about the surface equipment on the rig. The information about the surface equipment is used to determine the starting depth of well and the hanger depth for casing sections. (1:59)

Selecting rig equipment

To configure the surface equipment:

  1. Go to the Equipment Configuration page in the Setup > Surface Equipment section.
  2. Select the rig type and size from the options in the Rig Type and Rig Size fields. See "Field definitions".
  3. Select the type of BOP and MPD manifold on the rig.
  4. In the Rotating Control Device Type field, select the model of the RCD.
  5. For surface BOPs, select the source of the auxiliary pump.
  6. Enter the elevation of the rig. See "Rig Elevations".
  7. Click the Save Changes button () at the top of the screen.

The well's starting depth will be updated, based on the rig elevations entered. The layout of the equipment on the rig will also be updated, based on the equipment selected for the rig. See "Rig schematic".

Field definitions

The table below defines the fields for all rig types except coiled tubing units.

Field definitions for rig equipment
Field Definition
Rig Type

Classification for the type of rig used to drill the well.

  • Coiled Tubing Unit –
  • Compliant Tower – Drill barge that is towed by boat to the drill site.
  • Drill Ship – Self-propelled, oceangoing ship that is equipped with a derrick amidships and all associated drilling equipment.
  • Fixed Platform – Rig that rests on a platform above the water, supported by steel pillars.
  • Floater – Offshore drillstring structure that does not come into contact with the ocean floor, such as semi-submersible drilling rigs and drill ships.
  • Jack-Up – Offshore rig with legs that support the deck and rest on the sea floor at the drill site.
  • Land – Any drilling rig that is located on dry land.
Rig Size

Size of the rig, based primarily on the depth range of the rig.
Blow-out Preventer

Location of the BOP.

  • Subsea BOP – BOP is located below the water level. This option can not be used for land rigs.
  • Surface BOP – BOP is located at ground level or above the water level.
Rotating Control Device Type The model number of the Weatherford RCD on the rig. Not available for coiled tubing units.
Auxiliary Pump

Source of the auxiliary pump, if one is present. Auxiliary pumps can only be used with surface BOPs.

  • None – No auxiliary pump present.
  • Rig Pump – Uses the pump provided by the rig operator.
  • Weatherford – Uses the pump provided by Weatherford.
Kelly Bushing Elevation of the kelly bushing, relevant to MSL. For offshore rigs, the sea floor depth is calculated from the sum of the kelly bushing height and the water depth.
Ground Level Elevation of the ground level, relevant to MSL. This information is only required for land rigs.
Water Depth Distance between the sea floor and MSL. The sea floor depth is calculated from the sum of the kelly bushing height and the water depth. This information is only required for offshore rigs.
Inclination at Drill Floor Rig's deviation from the vertical direction of the well at the drill floor.
Azimuth at Drill Floor Rig's deviation from magnetic north at the drill floor.
Inclination at Ground Level Rig's deviation from the vertical direction of the well at ground level. This information is only required for land rigs.
Azimuth at Ground Level Rig's deviation from magnetic north at ground level. This information is only required for land rigs.
Inclination at Sea Bed Rig's deviation from the vertical direction of the well at the top of the sea bed. This information is only required for offshore rigs
Azimuth at Sea Bed Rig's deviation from magnetic north at the top of the sea bed. This information is only required for offshore rigs.
Surface Ambient Temperature Ambient temperature of the air at the surface
