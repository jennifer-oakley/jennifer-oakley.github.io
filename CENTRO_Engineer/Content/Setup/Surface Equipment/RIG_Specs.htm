About equipment specifications

The Setup > Surface Equipment > Piping and Instrumentation page includes the equipment specifications for several types of equipment, including the mud pumps, MPD manifold, RCD, BOP, and riser. The equipment specifications are used in several calculations and analyses. You can update the specifications by selecting the equipment in the rig layout. See "List of equipment specifications".

The Piping and Instrumentation page also contains a schematic of the surface equipment and piping on the rig. The equipment that appears in the rig schematic depends on the type of rig, the location of the BOP, and the equipment selected on the Equipment Configuration page. See "Rig schematic".

Entering rig limits

The specifications for the derrick, such as the hoisting capacity and torque rating, are used to calculate tension limits and torque limits for torque and drag calculations.

To enter the rig limits:

  1. Go to the Setup > Surface Equipment > Piping and Instrumentation page.
  2. Select the derrick in the rig schematic.
  3. In the panel that appears, enter the height and weight of the block.
  4. Enter the weight limits in the Nominal Hoisting Capacity and Operating Hoisting Capacity fields.
    • Nominal Hoisting Capacity is the recommended weight that can be lifted by the derrick.
    • Operating Hoisting Capacity is the maximum weight that can be lifted by the derrick.
  5. Enter the torque limits in the Nominal Torque Capacity and Operating Torque Capacity fields.
    • Nominal Torque Capacity is the recommended operating rig torque.
    • Operating Torque Capacity is the maximum allowable rig torque.
  6. Click the Save Changes button () to save your changes.

The derrick specifications will be used by CENTRO® Engineer to calculate tension limits and torque limits.

Updating equipment specifications

To update the equipment specifications:

  1. Go to the Piping and Instrumentation page in the Setup > Surface Equipment section.
  2. Select the equipment in the rig schematic. A panel with the equipment properties will appear.
  3. Update the equipment properties as needed.
  4. Click the Save Changes button () to save your changes.

The CENTRO® Engineer database will be updated with the new equipment specifications.

Pump properties

Properties of pumps
Field Definition
Pump State

Status of the pump

  • Aux – Auxiliary pump. Can only be used with a surface BOP, when an auxiliary pump is selected on the Equipment Configuration page.
  • Boost – Boost pump. Can only be used with a subsea BOP.
  • Disable – Pump is not active. Default pump state for all secondary pumps.
  • Main – Primary pump on the rig.
Efficiency Efficiency of the pump
Liner Size Diameter of the liner
Stroke Length Length of each pump stroke
Pump Source

Data source to use for the pump stroke rate.

  • External – Uses analog, WITS, or WITSML data for the stroke rate, as configured on the Setup > Mappings > Parameters page. See "Specifying the pump source".
  • Half Stroke Counter – Uses the stroke counter and counts half of the pulses received. You should select this option when using a proximity switch installed anywhere along the stroke.
  • Inverse Half Stroke Counter – Uses the stroke counter to invert the pulse wavelength and counts half of the pulses received. You should select this option when using a proximity switch installed anywhere along the stroke, and an inverted pulse signal is used. Typically, this applies to installations in which the metal piece triggering the sensor is three times wider than the sensor itself.
  • Low Pressure Flowmeter – Uses the low-pressure flow meter to measure the flow rate of fluids in systems operating at relatively low pressures. It reads data for mass flow, density, temperature, drive gain, strokes per minute, and total strokes as well. Available for Pump 1, Pump 2 and Pump 3 only. See "Specifying the pump source".
  • RPM – Measures rotations per minute to determine the stroke rate. Typically, this applies to proximity pump sensors that are mounted on a pump shaft.
  • Spare 1 – Uses the Spare 1 sensor to receive flow readings.
  • Spare 2 – Uses the Spare 2 sensor to receive flow readings.
  • Spare 3 – Uses the Spare 3 sensor to receive flow readings.
  • Spare 4 – Uses the Spare 4 sensor to receive flow readings.
  • Stroke Counter – Uses the stroke counter to count all of the pulses received. You should select this option when using a limit switch or a proximity switch installed at the end of the stroke.
N (# of Teeth) Number of teeth on the pump shaft. This field is only visible when the RPM option is selected for the pump source.
n1 (n1:1) First component of the gear ratio. This field is only visible when the RPM option is selected for the pump source.
n2 (n2:1) Second component of the gear ratio. This field is only visible when the RPM option is selected for the pump source.
Pump Type

Type of pump

  • Triplex – A reciprocating pump with three pistons or plungers.
  • Quadruplex – A reciprocating pump with four pistons or plungers.
  • Quintuplex – A reciprocating pump with five pistons or plungers.
Pump Output Volume of fluid passing through the pump, based on efficiency, liner size, stroke length, and pump type. This value is automatically calculated.
Max Pump HHP Maximum horsepower
Max Flow Rate Maximum allowable flow rate
Max Pump Pressure Maximum pressure rating
Pop Off Pressure Pressure at which the pressure valve will open to relieve pressure
Safety Factor Margin of error for the pump pressure. This value is automatically calculated from the values in the Max Pump Pressure and Pop Off Pressure fields.
