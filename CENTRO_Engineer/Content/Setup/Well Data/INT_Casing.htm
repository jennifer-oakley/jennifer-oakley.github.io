About casing and liner sections

Casing and liner sections are listed in the top portion of the Well Intervals page. There are four basic types of casing sections in CENTRO® Engineer: casing, tapered casing, liner, and production tubing.

  • Casing is cemented in place and extends to the surface.
  • Tapered casing is a casing section with multiple casing sizes, such as casing with the same OD and different IDs.
  • Liners are short casing strings, suspended inside an existing casing string.
  • Production tubing is tubing that is run inside the production casing.

Unlike casing, tapered casing and liners do not extend to the surface. Casing and liner sections are listed in order by decreasing OD, from the surface of the well to the bit. Production tubing sections, however, do not have to be listed in order by decreasing OD. Instead of using a single friction factor, axial and rotational friction factors are defined separately for each casing and liner section. The default hanger depth of casing, liner, and tubing sections is determined automatically, based on the type of rig and the rig elevations entered on the Surface Equipment > Equipment Configuration page. See "Rig Elevations".

To add a casing or liner section:

  1. Go to the Setup > Wells > Well Intervals page.
  2. Select the wellbore instance that you want to add a casing or liner section to.
  3. Click the Insert Item button (), then select the CS option in the menu that appears.
  4. In the panel that appears on the right, select the type of casing or liner from the drop-down list in the Type field.
  5. Select the section material from the list in the Material field.
  6. Enter the dimensions of the section in the OD, NW, and ID fields.
  7. Enter the depth at the bottom of the section in the SD field.
  8. For liner sections, enter the depth at the top of the section in the HD field.
  9. Click the OK button () to close the panel.
  10. Enter the friction factors for the section in the Friction Factor Axial and Friction Factor Rotational fields.
  11. Click the Save Changes button ().

The new section will be added to the list of casing and liner sections in the top half of the screen. The dimensions of the new section will appear in the Well View pane.

Field definitions

The table below defines the properties for casing and liner sections.

Properties of casing and liner sections
Field Definition
Type

Type of casing or liner

  • Conductor Casing is a short string of large-diameter casing that's used to keep the top of the wellbore open. Also called a boot.
  • Surface Casing is the first string of casing to be set in a well.
  • Intermediate Casing is the string of casing set in a well after the surface casing, and is used to prevent the wellbore from collapsing. Also called protection casing.
  • Production Casing is the last string of casing that is set in a well.
  • Liner is a short casing string, suspended inside an existing casing string.
  • Production Liner is the last string of liner that is set in a well.
  • Tapered Casing Section is a section with multiple casing sizes.
  • Production Tubing is tubing that is run inside the production casing. Unlike casing and liner, production tubing does not have to be listed in order by decreasing OD.
Material

Symbol for the material that the casing or liner is composed of and the grade of the material
OD

Outside diameter of the casing or liner section
NW

Nominal weight of the casing or liner section, calculated by dividing the mass of the section by the length of the section
ID

Inside diameter of the casing or liner section
SD

Setting depth, or the depth at the bottom of the section
HD

Hanger depth, or the depth at the top of the section
Friction Factor Axial

Axial friction factor for the casing or liner section
Friction Factor Rotational

Rotational friction factor for the casing or liner section
