About kick/loss zones

On the Setup > Wells > Kick/Loss Zones page, you can define the kick and loss zones in the wellbore formation. A kick/loss zone is a section of the formation where a kick or a loss is more likely to happen. There are two types of kick/loss zones in CENTRO® Engineer:

  • A Kick zone has a high pore pressure that increases the risk of a kick.
  • A Loss zone has a low fracture pressure that increases the risk of fluid loss.

Before you can run an influx simulation, you'll need to define the kick/loss zones for the well. You can also include an kick/loss zone in the settings for an underbalanced drilling analysis. See "Influx simulation" and "Displacement analysis".

Adding an influx zone

To add a kick/loss zone:

  1. Go to the Kick/Loss Zones page in the Setup > Wells section.
  2. Click the Add button (). A new row will appear at the bottom of the table.
  3. In the Name column, enter a name to identify the kick/loss zone.
  4. In the Type column, select the type of kick/loss zone.
  5. In the MD column, enter the measured depth at the top of the kick/loss zone.
  6. In the Length column, enter the length of the kick/loss zone.
  7. In the Drain-Off Radius column, enter the distance from the wellbore wall where pore pressure becomes constant.
  8. In the Reservoir Fluid column, select the type of fluid in the reservoir.
  9. In the Reservoir Fluid Density column, enter the density of the fluid in the reservoir.
  10. When you are finished adding kick/loss zones, click the Save Changes button ().

The changes will be saved to the CENTRO® Engineer database. The new kick/loss zone will appear in the settings for influx simulation and underbalanced drilling analysis.

Field definitions

The table below defines the kick/loss properties on the Setup > Wells > Kick/Loss Zones page.

Properties of kick/loss zones
Field Definition
Name Name of the kick/loss zone
Type

Type of kick/loss zone

  • A Kick zone has a high pore pressure that increases the risk of a kick.
  • A Loss zone has a low fracture pressure that increases the risk of fluid loss.
MD Measured depth at the top of the kick/loss zone, relative to the surface
Length Length of the kick/loss zone
Drain-Off Radius Distance from the wellbore wall at which pore pressure becomes constant
Reservoir Fluid Type of fluid in the reservoir
Reservoir Fluid Density Density of the fluid in the reservoir, at standard temperature and pressure

Using the Kick/Loss Zones page

The toolbar at the top of the screen on the Kick/Loss Zones page is used to add kick/loss zones. To export kick/loss zone data to a CSV file, press the Ctrl + S keys. To select the columns shown in the Kick/Loss Zones table, click the Show Columns button () in the first column of the table.

Toolbar buttons on the Wells > Kick/Loss Zones tab
Button Function

Discards all changes made since the last time you saved

Saves all changes
Adds a new kick/loss zone at the end of the table

The toolbar for each kick/loss zone is used to delete kick/loss zones.
