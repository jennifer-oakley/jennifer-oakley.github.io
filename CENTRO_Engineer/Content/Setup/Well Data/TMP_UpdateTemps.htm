About temperature data

There are two modes for entering temperature data:

  • In Temperature mode, you can enter the temperatures measured at each temperature point. The thermal gradients at each temperature point will be automatically calculated from the temperatures. See "Adding temperatures".
  • In Gradient mode, you can enter the thermal gradients along the wellbore formation. The temperatures at each point will be automatically calculated from the thermal gradients. See "Using thermal gradients".

The Temperature Mode button () button at the top of the screen allows you to switch between these two modes. When you're in Temperature mode, the heading for the second column in the table will be Temperature. When you're in Gradient mode, the heading will be GG.

Adding temperatures

To add formation temperatures:

  1. Go to the Setup > Wells > Temperatures page.
  2. If you're in Gradient mode, click the Temperature Mode button () to switch to Temperature mode.
  3. Click the Add button (). A new row will appear at the bottom of the table.
  4. In the TVD column, enter the TVD of the depth where the temperature was measured.
  5. In the Temperature column, enter the temperature for that depth.
  6. When you are finished adding temperatures, click the Save Changes button ().

As you add temperatures to the table, the chart at the bottom of screen will be updated with the new values. When you save your changes, the temperature data will be stored in CENTRO® Engineer's database.

Using thermal gradients

To add temperature data using thermal gradients:

  1. Go to the Setup > Wells > Temperatures page.
  2. If you're in Temperature mode, click the Temperature Mode button () to switch to Gradient mode.
  3. Click the Add button (). A new row will appear at the bottom of the table.
  4. In the TVD column, enter the TVD of the depth where the gradient was calculated.
  5. In the GG column, enter the thermal gradient for that depth.
  6. When you are finished adding data, click the Save Changes button ().

As you add thermal gradients to the table, the chart at the bottom of screen will be updated with the new temperatures. When you save your changes, the temperature data will be stored in CENTRO® Engineer's database.
