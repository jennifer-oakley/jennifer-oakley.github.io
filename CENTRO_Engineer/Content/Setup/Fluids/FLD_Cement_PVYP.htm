PV YP LSYP properties

About PV YP LSYP properties

The PV YP LSYP tab allows you to enter the rheological properties for a Bingham plastic cement. Bingham plastic cements are fluids that require a minimum shear stress to start and maintain flow. There are two primary parameters for the Bingham plastic rheology model: plastic viscosity and yield point. Plastic viscosity and yield point can be calculated from viscometer readings.

For a more accurate picture of the fluid's rheology, the PV YP LSYP tab also includes properties for low-shear yield point and gel strength. Gel strength can be read directly from a viscometer, and the low-shear yield point can be calculated from viscometer readings.

Using PV YP LSYP properties

To enter properties for a new cement:

  1. Go to the Setup > Fluids > Cement page to add a new cement. See "Adding cements".
  2. Go to the PV YP LSYP tab.
  3. In the PV field, enter the plastic viscosity of the cement.
  4. In the YP field, enter the yield point.
  5. In the LSYP field, enter the low-shear yield point.
  6. In the Gel Strength field, enter the 10-second gel strength.
  7. Click the Save Changes button () to save your changes.

When you save the cement, it will be added to the CENTRO® Engineer database. When you run an analysis, you'll be able to select the new cement in the analysis settings. However, you will not be able to edit the cement properties.

PV YP LSYP properties

On the PV YP LSYP tab, the fluid's rheology is defined using the parameters listed below.

PV YP LSYP properties
Field Name Definition
PV Plastic viscosity, a measure of shear stress that indicates the cement's resistance to flow.
YP Yield point, the minimum amount of shear stress required for the cement to flow.
LSYP Low-shear yield point, the yield point of the cement at a low shear rate.
Gel Strength 10 second gel strength, a measurement of the cement's ability to develop and retain a gel form. Gel strength is measured at a low shear rate, after the cement has set for a certain period of time.
