About underbalanced drilling fluids

Underbalanced fluids are lighter than most drilling fluids. They are used during underbalanced drilling to reduce the wellbore pressure below the formation pressure. Underbalanced fluids have liquid and gas phases, such as nitrogen or air.

CENTRO® Engineer currently supports three types of underbalanced drilling fluids:

  • Dust – Dry air is compressed and injected into the well, along with a small amount of oil to reduce corrosion.
  • Foam – A large amount of foaming agent is added to dry air, and the resulting bubbles bring the cuttings back to the surface.
  • Mist/Gasified – A small amount of foaming agent is added to dry air, creating fine particles of water and foam that bring the cuttings back to the surface.

The fluids used for underbalanced drilling are more environmentally friendly and cost effective than conventional drilling fluids. In addition, the threat of influxes from the reservoir is reduced. However, the pressure, velocity, and density of underbalanced fluids are more difficult to control.

Note

Underbalanced fluids cannot be used as the pump fluid. However, underbalanced fluids can be used to run an underbalanced drilling analysis in the Planning > Hydraulics section. See "Underbalanced drilling analysis".

Adding underbalanced fluids

To add an underbalanced fluid:

  1. Go to the Setup > Fluids > Underbalanced page.
  2. Click the Add button on the right side of the screen. A new tab will appear in the list of fluids.
  3. In the Type field, select the type of underbalanced fluid.
    • Dust – Dry air is compressed and injected into the well, along with a small amount of oil to reduce corrosion.
    • Foam – A large amount of foaming agent is added to dry air, and the resulting bubbles bring the cuttings back to the surface.
    • Mist/Gasified – A small amount of foaming agent is added to dry air, creating fine particles of water and foam that bring the cuttings back to the surface.
  4. In the Fill Color field, select a color to represent the dust in the Well View pane.
  5. In the Gas Type field, select the type of gas used for the gas phase of the fluid.
  6. In the Gas Density field, enter the specific gravity of the gas phase of the fluid.
  7. Enter a friction factor for the fluid.
  8. Enter the remaining properties of the fluid. See "Field definitions".
  9. Click the Save Changes button ().

The fluid will be stored in CENTRO® Engineer's database. When you run an underbalanced drilling analysis, you'll be able to select the new fluid in the analysis settings. See "Underbalanced drilling analysis". After you save the fluid, you will not be able to edit the fluid properties.

Field definitions

The table below defines the properties for dust fluids.

Field definitions for dust fluids
Field Name Definition
Type Type of underbalanced drilling fluid
Fill Color Color used to represent the underbalanced fluid in the Well View pane
Gas Type Type of gas used for the gas phase of the fluid, either dry air or nitrogen
Gas Density Specific gravity of the gas phase of the fluid, at the standard pressure and temperature
Friction Factor Override friction factor of the fluid, used when gas dominates the fluid flow

The table below defines the properties for foam fluids.

Field definitions for foam fluids
Field Name Definition
Liquid Density Density of the liquid phase of the fluid. Not available for dust fluids.
Fill Color Color used to represent the underbalanced fluid in the Well View pane
Type Type of underbalanced drilling fluid
Gas Type Type of gas used for the gas phase of the fluid, either dry air or nitrogen
Gas Density Specific gravity of the gas phase of the fluid, at the standard pressure and temperature
Friction Factor Override friction factor of the fluid, used when gas dominates the fluid flow.
Pipe Half-Life Length of time required for half of the foam to break down in a sample taken at the pipe inlet. This value is used to calibrate the friction factor of the fluid.
Pipe Height Average gas bubble size of the foam at the pipe outlet. This value is used to calibrate the friction factor of the fluid.
Annulus Half-Life Length of time required for half of the foam to break down in a sample taken at the annulus outlet. This value is used to calibrate the friction factor of the fluid.
Annulus Height Average gas bubble size of the foam at the annulus outlet. This value is used to calibrate the friction factor of the fluid.
Pipe Soap Exponent Component of the friction factor for the fluid as it passes through the drill pipe
Collars Soap Exponent Component of the friction factor for the fluid as it passes through the drill collars

The table below defines the properties for mist fluids.

Field definitions for mist fluids
Field Name Definition
Liquid Density Density of the liquid phase of the fluid. Not available for dust fluids.
Fill Color Color used to represent the underbalanced fluid in the Well View pane
Type Type of underbalanced drilling fluid
Gas Type Type of gas used for the gas phase of the fluid, either dry air or nitrogen
Gas Density Specific gravity of the gas phase of the fluid, at the standard pressure and temperature
Pipe Viscosity Viscosity of the fluid at the pipe inlet
Annulus Viscosity Viscosity of the fluid at the annulus outlet
Friction Factor Override friction factor of the fluid, used when gas dominates the fluid flow

Using the Underbalanced page

The toolbar at the top of the screen is used to manage the underbalanced fluids in your well.

Toolbar buttons on the Fluids > Underbalanced page
Button Function

Discards all changes made since the last time you saved

Saves all changes

The tabs on the right side of the screen are used to switch between underbalanced fluids. Each underbalanced fluid has its own tab, with a toolbar that is used to create, copy, and delete underbalanced fluids.

Toolbar buttons for underbalanced fluids
Button Function
Add Adds a new underbalanced fluid. See "Adding underbalanced fluids".

Creates a copy of the selected underbalanced fluid

Deletes the selected underbalanced fluid
