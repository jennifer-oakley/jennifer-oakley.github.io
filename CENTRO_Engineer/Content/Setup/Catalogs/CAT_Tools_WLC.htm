About wireline cable

Wireline cables are armored electrical cables that are used to lower wireline tools into the wellbore, as well as to transmit the data gathered by wireline tools. On the Setup > Catalogs > Custom Tools page, you can create custom wireline cable.

Creating wireline cable

To create new wireline cable:

  1. Go to the Setup > Catalogs > Custom Tools page.
  2. Select the catalog that you want to add a wireline cable to, and click the Insert Item button ().
  3. In the panel that appears on the right, go to the Tool field and select the Wireline Cable option.
  4. In the Tool Size field, select the nominal size of the wireline cable.
  5. In the Description field, enter a brief description of the wireline cable.
  6. Enter the dimensions of the part sections for the wireline cable.
  7. Update the remaining properties of the wireline cable as needed. See "Properties of wireline cable".
  8. Click the Save Changes button () to save your changes.

The wireline cable will be added to the selected catalog. The wireline cable will also appear in the list of available tools on the Setup > Work String page. See "Work strings".

Properties of wireline cable

The table below defines the properties for custom wireline cables in CENTRO® Engineer.

Properties of wireline cable
Section Field Definition
-- Manufacturer Manufacturer of the wireline cable
-- Tool Size

Nominal size of the wireline cable
-- Description

Name or description of the wireline cable
-- Length

Total length of the wireline cable. This value is calculated automatically.
-- Tool Weight Option

Method for entering the total mass of the wireline cable.

  • Calculated – The mass, weight in air, and weight in water of the wireline cableare calculated, based on the dimensions of the wireline cable. When the dimensions of the wireline cable are updated, the values for mass, weight in air, and weight in water will be automatically updated.

  • User-Defined Adjusted Weight – The weight in air of the wireline cable is a manually entered value. Mass and weight in water are automatically calculated, based on the weight in air of the wireline cable. When the dimensions of the wireline cable are updated, the value for weight in air will not be affected.

  • User-Defined Mass – The mass of the wireline cable is a manually entered value. Weight in air and weight in water are automatically calculated, based on the mass of the wireline cable. When the dimensions of the wireline cable are updated, the value for mass will not be affected.
-- Mass

Total mass of the wireline cable. When the Calculated option or the User-Defined Adjusted Weight option is selected in the Tool Weight Option field, this value is calculated automatically.
-- Weight in Air Total weight of the wireline cable when suspended in air. When the Calculated option or the User-Defined Adjusted Mass option is selected in the Tool Weight Option field, this value is calculated automatically and cannot be edited.
-- Weight in Water Total weight of the wireline cable when suspended in water. This value is calculated automatically and cannot be edited.
-- NW

Nominal weight of the wireline cable
-- Material

Symbol for the material that the wireline cable is composed of and the grade of the material.
-- Stretch Coeff Coefficient representing the length the cable will stretch when weight is applied to the cable
Body OD

Outside diameter of the part section.
ID

Inside diameter of the part section.
Length

Length of the part section.
Tension Limits Breaking Strength Overall breaking strength of the wireline cable
Working Tension

Working tension limit of the cable, expressed as a percentage of the cable breaking strength
Stuck Tool Working Working tension limit when pulling a stuck tool free, expressed as a percentage of the cable breaking strength
Weak Point Breaking Breaking limit of the weak point in the cable, expressed as a percentage of the cable breaking strength
Weak Point Low Low value in the cable head's weak point rating
Weak Point High High value in the cable head's weak point rating
Outer Wire BS Breaking strength of the outer wire in the wireline cable
Inner Wire BS Breaking strength of the inner wire in the wireline cable
Drum Crush Caution Multiple to use for determining if the tension while pulling out of hole has exceeded the caution threshold for drum crush. If the tension while pulling out of hole is greater than the cable's breaking strength multiplied by the Drum Crush Caution value, then the caution threshold has been exceeded.
Drum Crush Warning Multiple to use for determining if the tension while pulling out of hole has exceeded the warning threshold for drum crush. If the tension while pulling out of hole is greater than the cable's breaking strength multiplied by the Drum Crush Warning value, then the warning threshold has been exceeded.
Temperature Limits Temp Rating (1hr) Maximum temperature for one hour downhole
Temp Rating (8hr) Maximum temperature for eight hours downhole
Temp Rating (All) Maximum overall temperature
Other Parameters Inner Armor OD Outside diameter of the inner armor
Inner Armor Strength Breaking strength of the inner armor
Outer Armor ID Inside diameter of the outer armor
Outer Armor Strength Breaking strength of the outer armor
