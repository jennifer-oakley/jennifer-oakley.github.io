About monitoring reports

In the Reports > Reports section, you can create customizable reports that contain a detailed summary of the well and the drilling performance on the rig. There are two types of reports:

  • On-demand reports. You can create on-demand reports on the On-Demand page. On-demand reports are generated immediately, and will open in MS Excel. See "On-demand reports".
  • Scheduled reports. You can create scheduled reports on the Scheduled page. Scheduled reports can be generated at hourly, daily, and weekly intervals. See "Scheduled reports".

Both types of reports can include the same types of information, such as information about rig activities, friction factors, torque & drag effects, planned vs. actual comparisons, and hydraulics calculations. See "Report details".

Note

The Reports section is only available in Real-Time mode. See "Application modes".

Report details

The table below lists the information that can be included in a monitoring report.

Contents of a monitoring report
Main Section Sub-Section Explanation
Executive Summary Daily Operational Summary An end-of-day summary, as well as details about the job, well intervals, drilling fluid, average drilling parameters, and personnel for the selected report range
KPI Summary Time-based breakdown of rig activities and a summary of various performance indicators such as ROP, tripping speed, and connection time
BHA and Drillstring Information Configuration of the BHA used for the selected report range
Performance Analysis Operational Time Breakdown Detailed breakdown of the information from the KPI summary
Drilling KPIs Key performance indicators for rotary, slide, and dry rotary drilling operations
Tripping KPIs Key performance indicators for running in and pulling out
Connection KPIs Key performance indicators for weight-to-weight, slip-to-slip connection time while drilling, and slip-to-slip connection time while tripping
Torque & Drag Friction Factor Sensitivity Charts Friction factor sensitivity charts from real-time drillstring analysis, which include hookload and surface torque profiles
T&D Profiles at End-of-Day Well Depth Real-time drillstring analysis profiles for axial load, torque, contact force, and Von Mises stress
T&D Plan vs. Actual (PVA) Depth-based comparison of planned vs. actual surface torque and hookload, while drilling and rotating off-bottom
T & D – Model Vs. Actual (MVA) Time-based comparison of planned vs. actual surface torque and hookload, while drilling and rotating off-bottom
Hydraulics Pressure Distribution at Total Depth Pressure distribution throughout the wellbore at total depth
Hydraulic Profiles at Current Well Depth Depth-based summary of hydraulics and geomechanics model details and end-of-day hydraulics results
Model vs. Actual (MVA) SPP Time-based comparison of planned vs. actual standpipe pressure for all operations
Appendix Surveys List of survey points for the actual well path
Survey Inspection Depth-based representation of the survey data
Comments Any feedback entered for the selected day
