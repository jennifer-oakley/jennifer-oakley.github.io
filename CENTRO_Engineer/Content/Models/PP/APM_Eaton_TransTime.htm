Eaton Transient Time Method

The procedure used for estimation of the pore pressure from the sonic log based on Eaton’s correlation is defined as (Eaton, 1975)

Pn=ρgTVD

Where

Pn Normal hydrostatic formation pressure, [Pa]
TVD

True vertical depth [m];
g Gravitational acceleration [m/s2]
ρ Formation fluid density [kg/m3] (commonly water density)
Sv Overburden stress, [Pa]
a Calibration factor (from 0 to 1)
∆Tn Normal compaction transient time [μsec/m] (currently chosen by user)
∆T Compressional transient time from sonic log [μsec/m]

Vertical - Overburden stress is calculated using the integration of rock densities from surface to the depth of interest

Where:

PH Datum pressure, at the surface (Pa)
g Acceleration due to gravity (m2/s)
ρ(z) Bulk density of the overlying rock at depth z (kg/m3)
