Viewing detailed results

After you run A snapshot analysis, the Show Full Results button () will appear next to the Show Graphs button () in the top toolbar. This button is used to switch between the main view, which contains the analysis settings and the results summary, and the detailed analysis results.

To view the detailed results for Asnapshot experiment:

  1. Go to the Planning > Hydraulics > Snapshot page.
  2. Select the experiment that you want to view results for.
  3. Click the Show Full Results button () in the top toolbar.

The detailed results will appear on top of the analysis settings. To close the detailed results, click the Show Full Results button () again.

Jetting calculations

The Jetting tab contains charts and other visualizations for MacJet and Sand Jet tools. The Jetting tab is only visible when the selected work string includes a MacJet tool or a SandJet tool.

The Mac Jet section includes:

  • A graphic showing the distance between the MacJet nozzles and the casing wall, calculated from the inside diameter of the wellbore and the MacJet nozzle.
  • A chart showing the effectiveness of the nozzles when jetted into liquid or into gas. Additionally, the chart shows jetting configurations that exceed limits and are not recommended.
  • The pressure loss through the nozzle, with the pressure loss ranges visualized using a colored bar. The blue area represents the lower range, the green area represents the upper range, and the red area represents the maximum range.

The Sand Jet section includes:

  • A graphic showing the distance between the SandJet nozzles and the casing wall, calculated from the inside diameter of the wellbore and the MacJet nozzle.
  • A chart showing the perforation depth vs. time.
  • The jetting time required to perforate the wall.
  • The total jetting time required to reach the maximum depth.

Power curves

The Power Curve tab contains the performance chart for a through-tubing motor. The Power Curve tab is only visible when the selected work string includes a through-tubing motor.

  • The blue circle represents the operating point for the experiment, based on the operating parameters. The horizontal line that intersects with the operating point is the torque value, and the vertical line is the speed value.
  • The gray lines represent the hydraulic power curves for the motor, based on the motor specifications for torque, flow rate, and speed.
  • The green lines represent the flow band for the motor. The flow band plots the speed and torque points for each flow rate in the motor specification.
  • The blue lines represent the pressure band for the motor. The pressure band plots a horizontal line for each torque setting in the motor specification, starting at lower speed for the torque rating and ending at the upper speed.
