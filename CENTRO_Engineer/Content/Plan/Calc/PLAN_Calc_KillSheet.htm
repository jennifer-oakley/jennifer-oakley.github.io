About the kill sheet tool

The kill sheet tool on the Planning > Calculators > Kill Sheet page allows you to calculate the kill parameters required to shut in the well during an influx. The kill parameters calculated include stroke counts, recommended kill mud weight, and circulating pressures.

There are two different methods for calculating influx parameters:

  • The driller's method requires the influx to be circulated out of the well with the original mud weight. If the original mud weight is insufficient to balance formation pressure, the well is killed by circulating a heavier kill mud in a second circulation.
  • The wait and weight method only requires one circulation. The influx is circulated out of the well during the same circulation that the kill mud is pumped into the well. A schedule for drill pipe pressure must be calculated to ensure that drill pipe pressure is held constant until the kill mud returns to the surface.
Note

The Kill Sheet page is only available in Real-Time mode. See "Application modes".

Using the kill sheet tool

To use the kill sheet tool:

  1. Go to the Planning > Calculators > Kill Sheet page.
  2. In the Method field, select the calculation method you want to use.
  3. Select the well interval, work string, and fluid you want to use for the calculations.
  4. In the Bit Depth field, enter the measured depth of the bit when the influx occurred.
  5. In the Pump Output at 100% Efficiency field, enter the volume of each pump stroke when the pump is operating at maximum efficiency.
  6. In the Pump Efficiency field, enter the current efficiency of the pump.
  7. Enter the shut-in pressures, slow pump properties, and leak-off test properties. See "Inputs".

The calculation results will appear on the right side of the screen, including pump strokes, mud densities, and circulating pressures. The table in the lower portion of the results shows the decrease in circulating pressure as the kill mud is pumped from the surface to the bit. See "Results". The results will be automatically updated as the input values are entered.

Inputs

The table below defines the inputs on the Planning > Calculators > Kill Sheet page.

Field definitions for kill sheet inputs
Field Name Definition
Method

Method for killing the well.

  • Drillers – The influx will be circulated out of the well before circulating the kill mud into the well.
  • Wait & Weight – The mud in the pits will be weighted to the kill mud weight before circulating the influx out of the well.
Well Interval Well interval to use for the calculations
Work String Drillstring or casing string used for the calculations
Fluid Circulating fluid used for the calculations
Bit Depth MD of the bit at the time the influx occurred
Bit Depth TVD TVD of the bit at the time the influx occurred. This value is calculated automatically, and cannot be edited.
Drillstring Internal Volume Volume of the fluid inside the drillstring at the time the influx occurred. This value is calculated automatically, and cannot be edited.
Annular Volume Volume of the fluid inside the annulus at the time the influx occurred. This value is calculated automatically, and cannot be edited.
Pump Output at 100% Efficiency Volume of fluid that passes through the pump during each pump stroke, when the pump is operating at 100% efficiency
Pump Efficiency Efficiency of the pump
Surface Line Capacity Volume of fluid inside the flowlines at the surface. Only available for wait and weight calculations.
Shut In Drillpipe Pressure Difference between the pressure at the bottom of the well and the hydrostatic pressure in the drillpipe, measured when the well is shut in
Shut In Casing Pressure Difference between the formation pressure and the hydrostatic pressure in the annulus when an influx occurs
Slow Pump Rate Reduced pump rate used when circulating during an influx
Slow Pump Rate Pressure Pressure when pumping at a reduced pump rate
Leak-Off Test Mud Density Maximum density of the mud used to perform a leak-off test
Surface Leak-Off Test Pressure Maximum pressure at the surface during a leak-off test

Results

The table below defines the calculation results on the Planning > Calculators > Kill Sheet page. The table in the lower portion of the results shows the decrease in circulating pressure as the kill mud is pumped from the surface to the bit.

Field definitions for kill sheet results
Field Name Definition
True Pump Output Volume of fluid that passes through the pump during each pump stroke, at the current pump efficiency
Strokes to Pump Number of pump strokes required for the fluid to travel from the flowlines to the pump. Only available for wait and weight calculations.
Strokes from Surface to Bit Number of pump strokes required for the fluid to travel down the drillstring, from the surface to the bit
Strokes from Bit to Shoe Number of pump strokes required for the fluid to travel up the annulus, from the bit to the casing shoe
Strokes from Bit to Surface Number of pump strokes required for the fluid to travel up the annulus, from the bit to the surface
Max Allowed Mud Density Maximum density of the mud, based on the maximum mud density used during a leak-off test
Max Allowed Ann. Surf. Pressure Maximum pressure at the surface, based on the maximum mud density and maximum surface pressure during a leak-off test
Max Allowed Ann. Surf. Pressure w/ Kill Mud Maximum pressure at the surface when the kill mud is circulating, based on the maximum allowable annular surface pressure and the density of the kill mud. Only available for drillers calculations.
Kill Mud Weight Recommended density of the kill mud, based on the density of the current fluid and the shut-in drillpipe pressure
Initial Circulating Pressure Circulating pressure at the beginning of the influx
Final Circulating Pressure Circulating pressure after the influx is circulated out
