About the stuck pipe calculator

The stuck pipe calculator on the Planning > Calculators > Stuck Pipe page identifies the most likely cause for a stuck pipe, which helps you determine the best approach to freeing the stuck pipe.

The stuck pipe calculator examines the real-time data received immediately before and after the stuck pipe event. It uses the real-time data to answer four questions:

  • What was the pipe motion before the pipe became stuck? The answer to this question is determined by examining the rig activities that occurred from the start of selected time range until the stuck pipe event. The result is determined by the most common pipe movement during the time range.
  • Can the pipe move down after becoming stuck? The answer to this question is determined by analyzing changes in block speed from the start of the stuck pipe event until the end of the selected time range. The result is determined by comparing the original running speed at the stuck pipe event to a weighted average of the running speed during the time range.
  • Can the pipe rotate after becoming stuck? The answer to this question is determined by analyzing changes in rotary speed from the start of the stuck pipe event until the end of the selected time range. The result is determined by comparing the original rotary speed at the stuck pipe event to a weighted average of the rotary speed during the time range.
  • Can the fluid circulate after the pipe became stuck? The answer to this question is determined by analyzing changes in standpipe pressure from the start of the stuck pipe event until the end of the selected time range. The result is determined by comparing the original standpipe pressure at the stuck pipe event to a weight average of the standpipe pressure during the time range.

The stuck pipe calculator analyzes three possible stuck pipe mechanisms for each of these questions: packing off and bridging, differential sticking, and wellbore geometry. The probability of each answer and mechanism combination is rated on a scale of 0 to 2, where 0 means not likely and 2 means very likely.

Note

The Stuck Pipe page is only available in Real-Time mode. See "Application modes".

Using the stuck pipe calculator

To use the stuck pipe calculator:

  1. Go to the Planning > Calculators > Stuck Pipe page.
  2. In the Date/Time for Stuck Pipe Event field, enter the suspected date and time of the stuck pipe event.
  3. In the Range In Seconds field, select the time range you want to analyze. The calculator will use this time range to analyzed the data received immediately before and after the stuck pipe event.
  4. Click the Calculate button () at the top of the screen.

A graph showing the block speed, standpipe pressure, and rotary speed for the time range before and after the event will appear on the left side of the screen. You can use this graph to determine a more exact time for the stuck pipe event. The results table, which shows the calculated result for each question listed above, will appear on the right side of the screen. The result for the each question will be highlighted in dark gray, and the numbers in this row will be reflected in the Totals row. The most likely stuck pipe mechanism is the mechanism with the highest percentage.

Field definitions

The table below defines the fields in the results table for the stuck pipe calculator.

Field definitions for the stuck pipe results table
Question Field Definition
Pipe Motion Prior to Sticking? Moving Up The pipe was moving upwards during the time interval before the stuck pipe event. Picking up, pulling out, and back washing operations are included in this category.
Rotating Up The pipe was rotating upwards during the time interval before the stuck pipe event. Back reaming, and dry back reaming are included in this category.
Moving Down The pipe was moving downwards during the time interval before the stuck pipe event. Letting down, running in, and washing operations are included in this category.
Rotating Down The pipe was rotating downwards during the time interval before the stuck pipe event. Reaming, drilling, dry reaming, and dry rotary drilling are included in this category.
Static The pipe was not moving during the time interval before the stuck pipe event. Off-bottom inactive, circulating off-bottom, dry rotating off-bottom, circulating and rotating off-bottom, and in-slips operations are included in this category.
Pipe Motion After Sticking? Down Free Running speed after the stuck pipe event was at least 90% of the running speed at the time of the stuck pipe event, indicating that the pipe was able to move downwards freely.
Down Restricted Running speed after the stuck pipe event was less than 90% of the running speed at the time of the stuck pipe event, indicating that downward pipe movement was somewhat restricted.
Down Impossible Running speed after the stuck pipe event was 10% or less of the running speed at the time of stuck pipe event, indicating that downward pipe movement was impossible.
Pipe Rotation After Sticking? Rotate Free Rotation speed after the stuck pipe event was at least 90% of the rotation speed at the time of the stuck pipe event, indicating that the pipe was able to rotate freely.
Rotation Restricted Rotation speed after the stuck pipe event was less than 90% of the rotation speed at the time of the stuck pipe event, indicating that pipe rotation was somewhat restricted.
Rotation Impossible Rotation speed after the stuck pipe event was 10% or less of the rotation speed at the time of the stuck pipe event, indicating that pipe rotation was impossible.
Circulation Pressure After Sticking? Circulation Free Standpipe pressure after the stuck pipe event was at least 90% of the standpipe pressure at the time of the stuck pipe event, indicating that fluid was able to circulate freely.
Circulation Restricted Standpipe pressure after the stuck pipe event was less than 90% of the standpipe pressure at the time of the stuck pipe event, indicating that fluid circulation was somewhat restricted.
Circulation Impossible Standpipe pressure after the stuck pipe event was 10% or less of the standpipe pressure at the time of the stuck pipe event, indicating that fluid circulation was impossible.
-- Totals Sum of the results values in the highlighted rows for each stuck pipe mechanism
-- Percentage Ratio of the results values for each stuck pipe mechanism, compared to the sum of the results values for all stuck pipe mechanisms. The most likely stuck pipe mechanism is the mechanism with the highest percentage.
