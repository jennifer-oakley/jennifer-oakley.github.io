About power section specifications

The power section specification tool on the Planning > HyperLine™ Toolbox > Power Section Spec Sheet page is used to view the complete specifications for the power section of a drilling motor.

To use the power section specification tool:

  1. Go to the Planning > HyperLine™ Toolbox > Power Section Spec Sheet page.
  2. Select the power section size.
  3. Select the power section model.

The specifications for the selected power section will be shown in the Performance Specifications section and the Dimensional Specifications section. See "Specifications". The charts on the right will be updated to show the operating limits for the power section. See "Charts".

Charts

When you select a power section, the charts on the right side of the screen will be automatically updated.

  • The Output Power chart graphs hydraulic output against differential pressure at the minimum, maximum, and median flow rates for the power section.
  • The Rotational Speed chart graphs rotary speed and torque against differential pressure at the minimum, maximum, and median flow rates for the power section.

Both of these charts show the operating limits for the power section.

  • The green dashed line represents the safe boundary. The safe boundary is the minimum differential pressure at which the motor can be safely operated. Operating below this limit is not recommended.
  • The yellow dashed line represents the recommended boundary. The recommended boundary is the maximum differential pressure at which it is recommended to operate the motor. Operating the motor with parameters that fall between the green line and the yellow line results in the highest possible performance, with minimal risk of failures.
  • The red dashed line represents the high risk boundary. The high risk boundary is the maximum differential pressure at which the motor can be operated. Operating the motor with parameters that fall between the yellow line and the red line is within the specifications for the motor, but there is an increased risk of failures. Operating the motor with parameters that fall beyond this limit is outside the specifications for the motor and is not recommended.
