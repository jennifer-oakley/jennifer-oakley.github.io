About the power section evaluator

The power section evaluator tool on the Planning > HyperLine™ Toolbox > Power Section Evaluator page is used to calculate the power section's output under different combinations of differential pressure and flow rate. You can also use the power section evaluator to find the optimum operating conditions, generate the full specifications for the drilling motor, and visualize safe zones and risky zones.

To use the power section evaluator:

  1. Go to the Planning > HyperLine™ Toolbox > Power Section Evaluator page.
  2. Select the power section size and model.
  3. Select the elastomer type, then enter the relative fit.
  4. Enter the remaining operating parameters. See "Inputs".

The performance specifications and operating limits will be automatically populated from the CENTRO® Engineer catalog when you select the power section and model. The charts for mechanical power, rotary speed, and torque will appear on the right side of the screen. See "Results".

Inputs

The table below defines the calculation inputs on the Planning > HyperLine™ Toolbox > Power Section Evaluator page.

Calculation inputs for the power section evaluator
Section Field Name Definition
-- Power Section Size

Size of the power section on the drilling motor
-- Power Section Model

Motor size and model, with the rotor-stator lobes ration and the number of stages
-- Elastomer Type

Model of the elastometer on the drilling motor
-- Downhole Relative Fit

Percentage representing the absolute fit divided by the thickness of the minor stator. The thickness of the minor is determined by the difference of the catalog tube ID of the stator and the shop dimensions of the stator minor diameter.
Operating Conditions Flow Rate

Expected flow rate during the run
Differential Pressure

Maximum expected differential pressure through the motor
Max Circulating Temperature Maximum expected temperature of the circulating fluid

Results

The results for the power section evaluator include performance specifications, operating limits, and charts that graph differential pressure against mechanical power, rotary speed, and torque. These results are updated automatically as the calculation inputs are updated.

Performance specifications

The table below defines the inputs shown in the Performance Specifications section. These values are automatically populated from the CENTRO® Engineer catalog when you select the power section and model.

Performance specifications for the power section evaluator
Field Name Definition
Flow Range

Minimum and maximum recommended flow rates
Speed Range

Minimum and maximum recommended rotary speeds
Speed Ratio

Ratio between the motor's maximum speed and maximum flow rate.
Max. Differential Pressure

Maximum allowable pressure loss through the power section.
Torque at Max. DP

Amount of torque at the maximum allowable differential pressure.
Torque Ratio

Ratio between the maximum torque and the maximum differential pressure.
Nominal Max Power

Maximum allowed hydraulic power output

Differential pressure charts

The charts in the Differential Pressure section, on the right side of the screen, graph differential pressure against mechanical power, rotary speed, and torque.

The Differential Pressure vs. Mechanical Power chart graphs mechanical power and differential pressure for the minimum flow rate, maximum flow rate, and flow rate specified in the inputs.

  • The dotted blue lines represent the hydraulic horsepower at the minimum and maximum flow rates at which the motor can be operated.
  • The solid blue line represents the hydraulic horsepower at the flow rate entered in the inputs.

The Differential Pressure vs. RPM chart graphs rotary speed and differential pressure for the minimum flow rate, maximum flow rate, and flow rate specified in the inputs.

  • The dotted brown lines represent the rotary speed at the minimum and maximum flow rates at which the motor can be operated.
  • The solid brown line represents the rotary speed at the flow rate entered in the inputs.

The Differential Pressure vs. Mechanical Power and the Differential Pressure vs. RPM charts also graph the safe zone, recommended zone, and high risk zone from the Operating Limits table.

  • The dotted green line represents the boundary between the safe zone and the recommended zone, shown in the Operating Limits table.
  • The dotted yellow line represents the boundary between the recommended zone and the high risk zone.
  • The dotted red line represents the upper boundary of the high risk zone.
  • The black dot represents the intersection of the flow rate and differential pressure entered in the inputs. If the dot falls between the green and yellow dotted lines, the motor will be operating within the recommended zone. If the dot falls between the yellow and red dotted lines, the motor will be operating within the high risk zone.

The Differential Pressure vs. Torque chart graphs torque against differential pressure. The black dot represents the torque and differential pressure under the operating conditions specified in the inputs.

Operating limits

The Operating Limits table, below the Differential Pressure section, lists several flow rate increments that fall within the allowable flow rate range, shown in the performance specifications. For each flow rate increment, the Operating Limits table shows the safe, recommended, high risk, and out of specification ranges for differential pressure.

  • The Safe Zone range shows the minimum range of differential pressures at which the motor can be safely operated. However, operating within this range may result in sub-optimal performance.
  • The Recommended Zone range shows the recommended range of differential pressures. Operating the motor within this range results in the highest possible performance, with minimal risk of failures.
  • The High Risk Zone range shows the risky range of differential pressures. Although operating the motor within this range is within the specifications for motor, there is an increased risk of failures.
  • The Out of Spec range shows the high limit for the high risk zone. Operating the motor within this range is outside the specifications for the motor and is not recommended.

Using the Power Section Evaluator page

The toolbar at the top of the Power Section Evaluator page is used to automatically calculate the optimum conditions for the power section and generate reports.

Toolbar on the Power Section Evaluator page
Button Function
Finds the optimum flow rate and differential pressure for the power section. The values in the Flow Rate and Differential Pressure fields in the calculation inputs will be automatically updated with the calculated values.
Finds the optimum differential pressure for the power section, at the flow rate specified in the calculation inputs. The value in the Differential Pressure field in the calculation inputs will be automatically updated with the calculated value.

Generates a report of the power section calculation results. Only available after the analysis completes.
