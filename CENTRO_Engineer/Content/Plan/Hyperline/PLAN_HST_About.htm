HyperLine™ Toolbox

One of the most challenging aspects of utilizing downhole motors for drilling operations is understanding and predicting failure. The life of motors is critical, and in order to achieve optimum life, power sections, and bearing sections must be designed, selected and operated with knowledge of the factors that influence life and performance.

The HyperLine™ Toolbox section contains several powerful tools specifically designed to help operations in some crucial tasks.

  • The Motor Split Flow tool is used to predict the split flow through the motor bearing back and the Magnus RSS pads, based on the actual motor bearing measurements. See "Motor split flow tool".
  • The FIT Predictor tool is used to evaluate the rotor-stator fit on a drilling motor, based on the primary downhole variables. See "Fit predictor tool".
  • The DLS Predictor tool is used to calculate the expected dogleg severity for the drilling motor configuration. See "Dogleg severity predictor".
  • The Casing Pass-Thru calculator is used to calculate the minimum casing drift diameter for each bend angle available for each type of adjustable housing. See "Casing pass-thru calculator".
  • The Power Section Evaluator tool is used to calculate the power section's output under different combinations of differential pressure and flow rate. See "Power section evaluator".
  • The Power Section Spec Sheet tool is used to view the complete specifications for the power section of a drilling motor. See "Power section specifications".
Note

The HyperLine™ Toolbox section is only available in Real Time mode and Planning mode. See "Application modes".
