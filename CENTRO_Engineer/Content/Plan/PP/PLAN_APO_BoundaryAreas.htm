About boundary areas

The Bound tool allows you to add boundaries that represent depths of interest for the analysis. If there are no boundary areas defined, then the Results chart will show the results for all depths. You can add boundary areas to the Well Data, Offset Well, and Current Well charts.

Adding a boundary area

To add a boundary area:

  1. Select the experiment you want to edit, then click the Edit button ().
  2. Go to the Overburden tab.
  3. Click the Bound button () in the toolbar at the top of the screen.
  4. Go to the graph that you want to add a boundary area to.
  5. Locate the starting and ending TVD of the boundary area on the graph.
  6. Drag your mouse from the starting TVD to the ending TVD.

A blue box will appear on top of the graph to represent the depths of interest. When you are finished editing the experiment, click the Save Changes button ().

Editing boundary areas

To edit a boundary area:

  1. Click the Edit button ().
  2. Select the boundary area you want to edit.
  3. In the TVD from field, enter the new starting TVD.
  4. In the TVD to field, enter the new ending TVD.
  5. Click the Enter button () to update the graph.

The boundary area will move to the new position on the graph. When you are finished making changes, click the Close button ().
