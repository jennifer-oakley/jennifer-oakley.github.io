About calculating settings

On the Overburden tab, you can change the settings for some of the overburden calculations. This includes the coefficients for the Gardner method, which is used to calculate the formation density from logs for seismic, sonic, and check shot data. You can also change the density of the formation fluid, as well as the settings for calculating fluid density in the shallow section of the well.

Customizing the calculations

To change the advanced settings:

  1. Select the experiment you want to edit, then click the Edit button ().
  2. Go to the Overburden tab.
  3. Click the Settings button () in the lower left corner of the Results chart.
  4. In the Advanced Settings window, update the calculation settings as needed.
  5. Click the OK button () to update the graph with your changes.

The data in the Results chart will be updated to reflect the new calculation settings. When you are finished making changes, click the Close button (). When you are finished editing the experiment, click the Save Changes button ().

Settings for overburden calculations

The table below defines the calculation settings on the Overburden tab.

Field definitions for overburden calculation settings
Tab Field Defintion
Gardner Method α First coefficient used in the Gardner method. See "Gardner Method".
β Second coefficient used in the Gardner method. See "Gardner Method".
Formation Fluid Density Density of the formation fluid
Shallow Depths Mud Line Density Density of the fluid at the mudline
Shallow Section Depth Ending TVD of the shallow section
Interpolation Step Increment at which the fluid density within the shallow section is recalculated
Salt Corrections Name Name of the salt correction
Start TVD TVD at the top of the salt correction area
End TVD TVD at the bottom of the salt correct area
Density Adjusted density of the formation fluids in the salt correction area
