About well intervals

This video tutorial will teach you how to define the geometry of the casing sections and open hole sections along the trajectory of the well. (3:24)

The next step is to add the wellbore intervals. The Setup > Wells > Well Intervals page is used to define the geometry of the hole sections along the trajectory of the well. Casing sections are listed in the top portion of the Well Intervals page, and open hole sections are listed at the bottom. Instead of using a single friction factor, axial and rotational friction factors are defined separately for each section. The Well View pane shows a preview of the selected wellbore, including the casing sections, liner sections, and open hole sections.

There are three steps for defining the well intervals.

  • Define the open hole sections. See "Defining open hole sections".
  • Define the casing and liner sections. See "Defining casing sections".
  • Create additional wellbores as needed. See "Wellbores".

Defining open hole sections

To add an open hole section:

  1. Go to the Setup > Wells > Well Intervals page.
  2. Select the tab for the wellbore instance you want to update.
  3. Click the Insert Item button (), then select the OH option in the menu that appears.
  4. Double-click the MD field in the row that appears, then enter the depth at the bottom of open hole section.
  5. Enter the hole size in the Hole Diameter field.
  6. Enter the friction factors for the open hole section in the Friction Factor Axial and Friction Factor Rotational fields.
  7. Click the Save Changes button ().

The new open hole section will be added to the list of open hole sections in the bottom half of the screen. The dimensions of the new open hole section will appear in the Well View pane.

Properties of liner

To add a casing or liner section:

  1. Go to the Setup > Wells > Well Intervals page.
  2. Select the wellbore instance that you want to add a casing or liner section to.
  3. Click the Insert Item button (), then select the CS option in the menu that appears.
  4. In the panel that appears on the right, select the type of casing or liner from the drop-down list in the Type field.
  5. Select the section material from the list in the Material field.
  6. Enter the dimensions of the section in the OD, NW, and ID fields.
  7. Enter the depth at the bottom of the section in the SD field.
  8. For liner sections, enter the depth at the top of the section in the HD field.
  9. Click the OK button () to close the panel.
  10. Enter the friction factors for the section in the Friction Factor Axial and Friction Factor Rotational fields.
  11. Click the Save Changes button ().

The new section will be added to the list of casing and liner sections in the top half of the screen. The dimensions of the new section will appear in the Well View pane.

After you define the wellbore intervals, the next step is to enter the temperature data. See "Entering temperature data".
