About

I'm not the expert in the room. And that's why I'm exactly what you need.

15+Years
4+Concurrent projects

The best documentation isn't written by the person who knows the software inside out. It's written by the person who had to figure it out. I see your software the way your users do: as someone encountering it for the first time, looking for answers that aren't obvious. It's my job to figure things out, so your users don't have to.

I've spent 15 years doing exactly that in one of the most technically demanding software environments in the world. I came in knowing nothing about the oil and gas industry. I learned more than I ever expected, and built the documentation framework from the ground up in the process: tools, architecture, content, design, help systems, interactive demos, and video libraries.

Here's the thing most technical writers don't want to admit: nobody opens a user guide for fun. They open it because they're stuck, and they're already frustrated. My job isn't to document features for the sake of coverage. It's to get that person unstuck as fast as possible. Good documentation isn't just accurate. It's findable, readable, and actually solves the user's problem.
