About the well at depth tool

The well-at-depth tool on the Tools > Calculators > Well at Depth tab is used to calculate the parameters for the hydraulics model at any given measured depth in the well. Since sensors are not present throughout the entire well, the well-at-depth tool allows you to review critical hydraulics parameters anywhere in the well.

To use the well-at-depth tool:

  1. Go to the Well at Depth tab on the Tools > Calculators page.
  2. In the Measured Depth field, enter the measured depth that you want to use.

The hydraulics model will be calculated, using the specified depth and the current operating parameters. To refresh the hydraulics calculations, enter a new measured depth.

Tip

You can also use the Hydraulics indicator to review many of the parameters calculated by the hydraulics engine, such as pressure, temperature, and ECD. To open the Hydraulics Data window, click the Indicators button () in the Utilities toolbar and select the Hydraulics option. See "Hydraulics indicator".

Field definitions

The table below defines the values calculated on the Tools > Calculators > Well at Depth tab.

Calculated values for the well at depth tool
Field Name Definition
True Vertical Depth TVD of the well at the given measured depth
Annulus Pressure Total hydrostatic and frictional pressure in the annulus
Annulus Friction Pressure Frictional pressure in the annulus
Annulus ECD Equivalent circulating density of the fluid in the annulus
Annulus Temperature Temperature of the fluid in the annulus
Annulus Reference Density Bulk density of the annular fluid, at the reference pressure and temperature
Annulus Density Density of the fluid in the annulus
Downhole Mud Weight Equivalent static density at the bottom of the well
Pipe Pressure Total hydrostatic and frictional pressure in the drillstring
Pipe Friction Pressure Frictional pressure in the drillstring
Pipe Temperature Temperature of the fluid in the drillstring
Pipe Reference Density Bulk density of the fluid in the drillstring, at the reference pressure and temperature
Pipe Density Density of the fluid in the drillstring
Pipe Wall Temperature Temperature of the steel of the pipe wall
Annulus Wall Temperature Temperature of the annulus wall. In cased sections, this is the temperature of the steel in the casing. In openhole sections, this is the temperature of the formation wall.
