About trend lines

On the Pore Pressure tab, the Linear Trend Line and Semi-Log Trend Line tools allow you to add a trend line to normalize your log data. The normalized trend line is used as a baseline to calculate pore pressure by comparing the filtered log values against the hydrostatic gradient.

Linear trend lines are defined by the slope of the line and the base value at 0 TVD. Exponential trend lines are defined by the slope of the line and a curvature constant.

You can add multiple trend lines to the Offset Well and Current Well charts. However, trend lines can not overlap within the same chart.

Adding a normalized trend line

Linear and semi-log trend lines

To add a normalized trend line:

  1. Select the experiment you want to edit, then click the Edit button ().
  2. Go to the Pore Pressure tab and click on the trend line tool that you want to use.
    • To use a simple line, click the Linear Trend Line button ().
    • To use an exponential curve, click the Semi-Log Trend Line button ().
  3. Click on the graph you want to add a trend line to.
  4. Drag your mouse to connect two points in the graph to draw the trend line.

Victus™ Simulation will calculate the pore pressure at each filtered point along the trend line, and the calculated pore pressures will be automatically graphed in the Results chart. When you are finished editing the experiment, click the Save Changes button ().

Editing a normal trend line

To edit a normal trend line:

  1. Click the Edit button (), then click on the trend line you want to edit.
  2. In the window that appears, update the line's endpoints as needed.
    • TVD 1 is the TVD of the line's starting point.
    • TVD 2 is the TVD of the line's ending point.
  3. If the line is a linear trend line, update the slope and base value for the line.
    • K is the slope of the line.
    • A is the calculated value at 0 TVD.
  4. If the line is an exponential trend line, update the slope and curvature of the line.
    • K is a constant that effects the curvature of the line.
    • A is the slope of the line.
  5. Click the Enter button () to update the graph..

The trend line will move to the new position. The results for pore pressure will be recalculated and graphed in the Results chart. When you are finished making changes, click the Close button () to close the window.
