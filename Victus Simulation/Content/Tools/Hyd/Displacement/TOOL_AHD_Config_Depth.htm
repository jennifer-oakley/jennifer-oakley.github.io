About depth-based operations

Depth-based operations simulate displacement based on the top depth of the fluid column when you start pumping or circulation. The simulation calculates the volume required to reach the last casing shoe, based on the depth of the top of the fluid column. There are two types of depth-based operations:

  • Pumping – Estimates the displacement that occurs when pumping in a new fluid.
  • Circulation – Estimates the displacement that occurs when circulating fluids through the well.

When you configure the settings for a depth-based operation, you can also choose which fluid volume you want to calculate.

  • The Annular Volume option calculates the fluid volume required to reach the annulus.
  • The Open Hole Volume option calculates the fluid volume required to fill the open hole section.

Adding a depth-based operation

To add a depth-based operation:

  1. Click the Add Operation button () to add an operation to the analysis.
  2. In the Operation field, select the type of operation you want to simulate.
    • Pumping – Estimates the displacement that occurs when pumping in a new fluid.
    • Circulation – Estimates the displacement that occurs when circulating fluids through the well.
  3. In the Type field, select the Depth Based option.
  4. Select the fluid volume that you want to be calculated.
    • To calculate the volume required to reach the annulus, select the Annular Volume option.
    • To calculate the volume required to fill the open hole section, select the Open Hole Volume option.
  5. In the Fluid Top Depth field, enter the measured depth of the top of the fluid column.
  6. In the Operating Parameters section, go to the Fluid field and select the next fluid that will be pumped into the well. This setting is only available for pumping operations.
  7. Enter the flow rate, top drive speed, and other operating parameters.
  8. In the Operational Limits section, enter the minimum flow rate and pressure high limit.
  9. If the work string contains tools with nozzles, go to the Split Flow Components section to configure the flow settings for the tool.

During the analysis, the operation will be calculated in the order that it is listed.

Field definitions

The table below defines the settings required for a depth-based operation.

Settings for depth-based operations in a displacement analysis
Section Field Definition
-- Work String Depth

Depth at the end of the work string
-- Operation

Operation that you want to simulate fluid displacement for.

  • Pumping – Estimates the displacement that occurs when pumping in a new fluid.
  • Circulation – Estimates the displacement that occurs when circulating fluids through the well.
-- Annular Volume Calculates volume of fluid required to reach the annulus
-- Open Hole Volume Calculates the volume of fluid required to fill the openhole section
-- Fluid Top Depth Measured depth of the top of the fluid column
-- Volume Pumped Amount of fluid that will be pumped into the well. This value is calculated automatically and cannot be edited.
Operating Parameters Fluid

Next fluid that will be pumped into the well. This field is only used for pumping operations.
Pump Flow Rate

Expected flow rate through the pumps
Aux/Boost Flow Rate

Flow rate for the fluid from the auxiliary pumps and boost lines
TDS

Top drive speed for the analysis
Annulus Surface Pressure Surface backpressure at the choke that's applied to the annulus
Surface Line Friction

Pressure due to friction in the surface lines
Operational Limits Min Flow Rate

Minimum flow rate required to operate the work string, due to the operating requirements of the drilling motor or other tools
Pressure High Limit

Maximum anticipated surface backpressure
Split Flow Components Tool

Name of the configurable tool
Flow Path

Flow path through the nozzles on the tool. The options available vary, depending on the type of tool.
Discharge

Efficiency of the fluid discharge through the component. This value is automatically populated from the pump properties on the Setup > Surface Equipment page.
