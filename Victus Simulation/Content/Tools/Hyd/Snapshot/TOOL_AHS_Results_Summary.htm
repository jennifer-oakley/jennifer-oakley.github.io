Warnings

If the limits of pore pressure, fracture pressure, or flow rate are violated during the analysis, a warning message will appear at the top of the results summary. The table below shows a list of possible warnings, along with recommended solutions.

List of warning messages
Warning Message Recommended Solutions
ECD violates PPG/FPG window
  • If the PPG limit is violated, use a mud with a higher mud weight and/or increase surface backpressure.
  • If the FPG limit is violated, use a mud with a lower mud weight and/or decrease surface backpressure
ESD violates PPG/FPG window
  • If the PPG limit is violated, use a mud with a higher mud weight and/or increase surface backpressure.
  • If the FPG limit is violated, use a mud with a lower mud weight and/or decrease surface backpressure
ECD + Cuttings violates PPG/FPG window
  • If the PPG limit is violated, use a mud with a higher mud weight and/or increase surface backpressure.
  • If the FPG limit is violated, use a mud with a lower mud weight and/or decrease surface backpressure
Pump flow rate is less than the min flow rate required to clean the hole Increase the flow rate specified in the analysis settings.
Flow rate is greater than max allowed pump flow rate
  • Decrease the flow rate specified in the analysis settings.
  • Verify the max flow rate shown for the pumps on the Setup > Surface Equipment > Piping and Instrumentation tab. See " Pump configuration".
Work done by pump is greater than max allowed work
  • Decrease the flow rate specified in the analysis settings.
  • Verify the max pump HHP shown for the pumps on the Setup > Surface Equipment > Piping and Instrumentation tab. See " Pump configuration".
Pump pressure is greater than max allowed pump pressure
  • Use a mud with a higher mud weight and/or increase surface backpressure.
  • Verify the max pump pressure shown for the pumps on the Setup > Surface Equipment > Piping and Instrumentation tab. See " Pump configuration".
Flow rate is less than the minimum flow rate required to operate tool
  • Increase the flow rate specified in the analysis settings.
  • Verify the minimum flow rate shown for the tool on the Setup > Work String page. See "Work strings".
Flow rate is greater than the maximum flow rate required to operate tool
  • Decrease the flow rate specified in the analysis settings.
  • Verify the maximum flow rate shown for the tool on the Setup > Work String page. See "Work strings".
Reamer opened. Critical flow rate exceeded. Decrease the flow rate specified in the analysis settings.
Flow rate through turbine generator is above operating flow rate
  • Decrease the flow rate specified in the analysis settings.
  • Verify the turbine generator flow range selected for the Magnus RSS on the Setup > Work String page. See "Work strings".
Flow rate through turbine generator is below operating flow rate
  • Increase the flow rate specified in the analysis settings.
  • Verify the turbine generator flow range selected for the Magnus RSS on the Setup > Work String page. See "Work strings".
Flow rate through turbine generator is within 10% of the upper operating flow range
  • Decrease the flow rate specified in the analysis settings.
  • Verify the turbine generator flow range selected for the Magnus RSS on the Setup > Work String page. See "Work strings".
Flow rate through turbine generator is within 10% of the lower operating flow range
  • Increase the flow rate specified in the analysis settings.
  • Verify the turbine generator flow range selected for the Magnus RSS on the Setup > Work String page. See "Work strings".

Added gas summary

The Added Gas Summary section contains a summary of the flow rates and pressure affected by the gas component of the fluid. This section is only available when the fluid for the selected analysis includes a gas component, or when the work string contains a thru-tubing motor.

Calculated values for the gas summary
Value Definition
Gas Flow Flow rate of the gas through the work string
Total Flow Combined flow rate of the liquid and the gas
% Gas Percentage of the gas in the total flow rate
Pump Pressure Pressure at the pump on surface
Tq Dp Over Motor Relative difference between the pump pressure at the surface and pump pressure when off bottom, when torque equals zero
Motor Free Running Loss Pressure required to drive fluid through the motor with no load, at the flow rate specified in the analysis settings
% Gas Into Motor Percentage of gas in the total flow through the motor
Total Flow Into Motor Combined flow rate of the gas and the liquid through the motor. Typically, a higher annular pressure decreases the flow through the motor, and an increased gas flow rate will increase the flow through the motor.
Annulus Pressure at Motor Pressure inside the annulus at the motor

Split flow components summary

The Split Flow Component Summary section contains a summary of the pressures affecting the split flow components in the work string. A split flow component is a tool that diverts flow between the annulus and the pipe before the end of the work string. This includes tools such as drill bits, reamers, and circulation subs.

Summary results for split flow components
Column Definition
Tool Name of the configurable tool
Flow Split Percentage of the flow that has split to the annulus
Noz. P. Drop Pressure loss through the bit nozzles
Diff. Pressure at Top Differential pressure at the top of the tool
BAP Diff. Pressure Bore and annular pressure, calculated using the differential pressure at the location of the BAP sensor. Only calculated when the BHA contains a HEL-BAP LWD tool.

Pressure drop distribution

A table showing the distribution of circulating pressure losses is shown in the Pressure Drop Distribution section. Victus™ Simulation calculates circulating pressure losses across each work string component, as well as the following pressure losses:

  • Annulus is the circulating pressure loss across the annulus.
  • Coiled Tubing Surface is the circulating pressure across the coiled tubing that is still in the drum. Only available for work strings with coiled tubing as the top component in the work string.
  • Inlet Surface Equipment is the circulating pressure loss across the surface connections, such as the standpipe and Kelly hose.
  • U Tube is the pressure loss due to density differences in the pipe and the annulus.
  • SPP is the pressure through the standpipe, which is the sum of surface backpressure, circulating pressure losses in the annulus and drillstring, circulating pressure loss at the surface, and pressure loss in the U-tube.
