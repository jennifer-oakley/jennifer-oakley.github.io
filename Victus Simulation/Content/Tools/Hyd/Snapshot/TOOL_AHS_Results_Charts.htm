Viewing analysis charts

After you run A Snapshot analysis, the Calculate button () in the bottom toolbar will be replaced with the Show Graphs button (). This button is used to switch between the main view, which contains the analysis settings and the results summary, and the charts view.

To view the charts for A Snapshot experiment:

  1. Go to the Snapshot tab on the Tools > Hydraulics page.
  2. Select the experiment that you want to view results for.
  3. Click the Show Graphs button () in the bottom toolbar.

The charts will appear on top of the analysis settings. To close the analysis charts, click the Show Graphs button () again.

Viewing chart legends

By default, the chart legends are not shown. To pin the legends to the charts:

  1. Hover your mouse over the bottom right corner of the graph index, on the right side of the analysis charts.
  2. In the toolbar that appears, click the Show/Hide Legend button ().

The legend for each chart will appear in the top right corner of the graph.

Adding charts

In addition to the charts shown by default, there are more charts available. To view these charts:

  1. Hover your mouse over the bottom right corner of the graph index, on the right side of the analysis charts.
  2. In the toolbar that appears, click the Add Graph Page button () to view the additional charts that are available.
  3. In the menu that appears, click on the chart you want to view.

The selected chart will appear last in the charts view, on the right side of the current charts.

Changing graph settings

You can change the appearance of a chart in the Graph Settings window. Each chart has its own settings, so changing the appearance of one chart will not affect how another chart looks.

To customize the default graph settings:

  1. Hover your mouse over the bottom right corner of the chart you want to customize. Three buttons will appear on top of the chart.
  2. Click the Edit button () to open the Graph Settings window.
  3. In the menu at the top of the window, select the name of the chart series that you want to customize.
  4. Change the options for the chart series as desired.

As you make changes to the chart, your changes will be automatically saved and updated. When you are finished making changes, click the Close button () at the top of the window.

List of analysis charts

The charts listed below are available after you run an analysis on the Snapshot tab.

Analysis charts for a snapshot analysis
Chart Name Description Chart Sample
Velocity Shows changes to the velocity of the fluid in the annulus and the drillstring throughout the well.
ECD

Shows changes to equivalent static density and equivalent circulating density throughout the well. The chart shows limits that represent formation pressure limits. If a line falls within the shaded areas, formation limits have been exceeded.

  • The lines represent ESD and ECD.
  • The gray area represents the pore pressure gradient in the formation.
  • The light gray area represents the wellbore stability gradient in the formation.
  • The orange area represents the fracture pressure gradient in the formation.
  • The green and pink dots represent results from leak-off tests and formation integrity tests.
Temperature Shows changes in the fluid temperature in the drillstring and the annulus, as well as changes in the formation fluid temperature.
Hole Cleaning

Shows changes in the concentration of cuttings, bed height, and the hole cleaning index.

  • The red line represents cuttings concentration, determined by ROP, bit size, and the ratio between the pipe OD and the hole ID.
  • The blue line represents bed height, determined by the ratio between the flow rate and the critical flow rate required to clean the hole.
Pressure

Shows changes to the total pressure in the annulus and the drillstring throughout the well. The chart shows limits that represent formation pressure limits. If a line falls within the shaded areas, formation limits have been exceeded.

  • The lines represent drillstring and annular pressure.
  • The gray area represents the pore pressure in the formation.
  • The light gray area represents the wellbore stability.
  • The orange area represents the fracture pressure in the formation.
  • The green and pink dots represent results from leak-off tests and formation integrity tests.
CTR

Shows changes to the cuttings transport ratio throughout the well.

  • The blue line represents the cuttings transport ratio, which is the ratio of the cuttings velocity to the annulus fluid velocity.
  • The dashed green line represents the minimum cuttings transport ratio. For adequate hole cleaning, the blue line should fall on the right side of the CTR cuttings transport ratio limit.
Differential Pressure

Shows the changes in differential pressure throughout the well, which is the difference between annular pressure and drillstring pressure. The chart also shows limits that represent the pressure limits of the tools in the work string. If a line falls within the shaded area, work string pressure limits have been exceeded.

  • The blue line represents the differential pressure.
  • The orange area represents the burst capacity of the tools in the work string.
  • The gray area represents the collapse capacity of the tools in the work string.
Inclination Shows changes in the wellbore inclination along the length of the well.
Rheology Shows changes in the fluid's plastic viscosity, yield point, and LSYP low shear yield point throughout the well.
