About the Volumes indicator

The Volumes window contains counters for the fluid and pipe volumes throughout the well. There are three tabs in the Volumes window:

  • The Total tab shows a running counter of the total volumes in the well, annulus, and drillstring. See "Total volumes".
  • The Section tab calculates the volume of the fluid in a particular section of well. See "Section volumes".
  • The Time tab shows the fluid gain or loss within a certain time period. See "Gain and loss volumes".

To open the Volumes window, click the Indicators button () in the Utilities toolbar and select the Volumes option.

Total volumes

The table below defines the calculated values shown on the Volumes > Total tab.

Calculated volumes on the Total tab of the Volumes window
Field Name Definition
Hole Total volume of the fluid in the annulus and the drillstring, as well as the volume of the fluid displaced by steel. This value is the sum of the Annulus, Pipe, and Steel fields.
Annulus Total volume of the fluid in the annulus
Pipe Total volume of the fluid in the drillstring
Steel Total volume of the drillstring, calculated as the fluid displaced by the drillstring
Riser Total volume of the fluid in the riser
Annulus above bit Volume of the annular fluid above the drill bit
Surface pipe Total volume of the fluid in the surface pipe, from the RCD to the MPD manifold

Section volumes

On the Volumes > Section tab, you can calculate the fluid volumes in a particular section of the well. To calculate section volumes:

  1. Click the Indicators button () in the Utilities toolbar, then select the Volumes option.
  2. In the Volumes window, go to the Section tab.
  3. In the Start Depth field, enter the measured depth at the top of the section.
  4. In the End Depth field, enter the measured depth at the bottom of the section.
  5. Click the Calculate button () at the top of the Volumes window.

The fluid volumes will be calculated for the well section. The results will appear below the Start Depth and End Depth fields.

Gain and loss volumes

On the Volumes > Time tab, you can calculate the volume of fluid gained or lost within a certain time period. To calculate gain/loss volumes:

  1. Click the Indicators button () in the Utilities toolbar, then select the Volumes option.
  2. In the Volumes window, go to the Time tab.
  3. In the Start Time field, enter the date and time at the beginning of the time period.
  4. In the End Time field, enter the date and time at the end of the time period.
  5. Click the Calculate button () at the top of the Volumes window.

The fluid gain/loss will be calculated for the time period. The results will appear in the Gain/Loss field. To reset the time frame to the current time, click the Refresh Time button ().
