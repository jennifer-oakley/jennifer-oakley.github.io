About the Capture Video utility

The Capture Video utility allows you to record a video of Victus™ Simulation. Recordings can be a useful for recording real-time data when problems are encountered downhole. They can also be used for training rig personnel, client presentations, and post-well reviews.

The Capture Video utility records all activity on your screen, including activity in other applications. The recording also includes audio, which will be recorded using your default recording device. During a recording, a red dot will appear on the Capture Video button () to indicate that the recording utility is active.

Videos are stored in your Victus folder, under the C:\Users\[UserName]\Documents\Victus\Videos directory. The name of each video includes the date and time that the video was recorded.

Recording a video

To record a video using the Record Video utility:

  1. Before you begin the recording, review the list of tips for recording videos. See "Tips for recording videos".
  2. Click the Capture Video button () in the Utilities toolbar.
  3. When you are ready to end the recording, click the Capture Video button () again.

The completed recording will be saved to your Victus folder, under the Videos directory.

Tips for recording videos

Before you begin a recording, refer to the following list of tips to improve your recording.

  • If you have multiple monitors, make sure that Victus™ Simulation is on your primary screen. The Record Video utility only records the activity on your primary screen.
  • All activity on your primary screen will be recorded, including activity in other applications. Minimize or close any applications that you don't want to be included in the recording.
  • For the best audio quality, use a microphone or headset during the recording. Built-in microphones typically record more ambient noise than standalone and headset microphones.
  • The Record Video utility uses your default recording device to record audio. If you are using a headset microphone, you may need to change the default recording device in the Sound control panel. See "Setting the default recording device".
  • Some microphones have enhancements to improve audio quality by filtering out background noises. You can check your microphone settings in the Sound control panel.
  • If you don't want to include audio, mute your microphone. This will prevent ambient noise from being recorded. Most microphones have a mute button somewhere on the microphone. If not, you can mute your microphone in the Control Panel on your computer. See "Muting the microphone".
  • Most computers have a built-in microphone. Even if you don't connect a microphone to your computer, ambient noise may still be recorded. If you want to include audio, check the microphone settings for noise cancellation or filtering options. If not, you can mute the built-in microphone. See "Muting the microphone".

Setting the default recording device

To change the default recording device:

  1. Go to the Start menu on your computer, then open the Control Panel.
  2. Go to the Hardware and Sound category, then open the Sound control panel.
  3. On the Recording tab, select the microphone you want to use.
  4. Click the arrow next to the Set Default button at the bottom of the window.
  5. Select the Default Device option, then click OK to close the Sound control panel.

The microphone will be designated as the default device in the Sound control panel. When you begin the recording, the microphone you selected will be used to record audio.

Muting the microphone

To mute your microphone:

  1. Go to the Start menu on your computer, then open the Control Panel.
  2. Go to the Hardware and Sound category, then open the Sound control panel.
  3. On the Recording tab, select your default microphone and click Properties.
  4. In the window that opens, go to the Levels tab.
  5. Click the speaker button () next to the microphone volume, then click OK to close the properties window.

The icon on the speaker button () will change to indicate that the microphone is muted. When you begin the recording, no sound will be recorded.
