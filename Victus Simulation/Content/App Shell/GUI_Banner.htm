About notifications

The Notifications banner displays alerts when hardware or system problems are encountered. It also shows the well name, rig name, and version number for Victus™ Simulation.

When a limit is exceeded, the banner changes color to yellow. An orange warning symbol () will also appear in the banner. See "Active alarms". When there is a problem with the well, such as a kick or a loss, the banner changes color to orange or red. See "Banner messages".

Real-time controls

In Real-Time mode, Victus™ Simulation can acquire new data from WITS, modbus, or WITSML data sources and perform real-time hydraulics calculations. When Victus™ Simulation is in Real-Time mode, the toolbar at the top of the screen allows you to start and stop acquiring data.

You can also change the speed at which hydraulics model and choke calculations are performed. When you increase the calculation rate, data will still be acquired in real-time. The clock time and chart times will also run at the normal speed. To change the calculation rate, click on the clock and select a new calculation rate from the menu that appears.

  • x0 – Pauses data acquisition and model calculations.
  • x1 – Performs hydraulics model calculations and choke calculations in real-time.
  • x2 – Increases speed of hydraulics model calculations to five times faster and choke calculations to two times faster.
  • x3 – Increases speed of hydraulics model calculations to ten times faster and choke calculations to three times faster.
  • x4 – Increases speed of hydraulics model calculations to 20 times faster and choke calculations to four times faster.

To switch to Real-Time mode, go to the File > Projects page. See "Switching between modes".

Playback controls

In Playback mode, Victus™ Simulation replays the well data acquired in Victus™ Simulation or Victus™ Operation. The well configuration and rig states will change as they were changed in real-time. In Playback mode, you can speed up the data and fast-forward to a specific point of time. However, the well data can't be edited when you're using Playback mode.

When Victus™ Simulation is in Player mode, the toolbar at the top of the screen allows you to adjust the rate at which the data is replayed. To replay data from a different date, click the Select Date button (). Use the slide control to skip to another section of the data for that date.

To change the playback rate, click on the clock and select a new playback rate from the menu that appears.

  • x0 – Pauses playback.
  • x1 – Plays the data in real-time.
  • x2 – Plays the data at double speed.
  • x3 – Plays the data at triple speed.
  • x4 – Plays the data at quadruple speed.

To switch to Playback mode, go to the File > Projects page. See "Switching between modes".

Banner messages

There are three situations that will cause the banner at the top of the screen to flash orange or red:

  • Emergency shutdown. During an emergency shutdown, the banner will flash red. An emergency shutdown is triggered when surface backpressure exceeds 98% of the high limit. See "Pressure Limit control".
  • Kick detected. When a kick is detected, the banner will flash orange. Kicks are detected and controlled using the settings on the Setup > Control Algorithms > Kick tab. See "Kick algorithm".
  • Loss detected. When a loss is detected, the banner will flash orange. Losses are detected and controlled using the settings on the Setup > Control Algorithms > Loss tab. See "Loss algorithm".

The table below defines the warnings that may appear in the banner at the top of the screen:

Circulating Influx out - Influx is reaching surface A Kick is being automatically controlled. The influx is circulating through the well and is located in the annulus, near the surface. SPP mode is being used to maintain constant SPP while the influx circulates out of the well.
Circulating Kill Mud - Kill mud is at surface A Kick is being automatically controlled. The kill mud has finished circulating through well and is currently located at the surface. When prompted, you'll need to confirm that the kill mud has circulated out of the well. You'll also need to re-enable kick/loss detection so that Victus™ Simulation will return to monitoring for new kicks.
Circulating Kill Mud - Kill mud is coming up A Kick is being automatically controlled. The kill mud is circulating through the well. The mud has passed the bit and is currently located in the annulus. Victus™ Simulation has automatically switched to SPP mode while the kill mud circulates through the annulus.
Circulating Kill Mud - Kill mud is going down A Kick is being automatically controlled. The kill mud is circulating through the well, and the mud is currently located in the drillstring. Victus™ Simulation has automatically switched to SBP mode while the kill mud circulates through the drillstring.
Circulating Kill Mud - Kill mud is reaching surface A Kick is being automatically controlled. The kill mud is circulating through the well. The mud is currently located in the annulus, near the surface. SPP mode is being used to maintain constant SPP while the kill mud is circulating through the annulus.
Circulating Kill Mud - Kill Mud is reaching the Bit A Kick is being automatically controlled. The kill mud is circulating through the well. The mud is currently located in the drillstring, near the bit. SBP mode is being used to maintain constant SBP while the kill mud circulates through the drillstring.
Emergency shutdown - Confirm! Victus™ Simulation has gone into an emergency shutdown because surface backpressure has exceeded 98% of the high limit. The chokes are being fully opened. Victus™ Simulation has automatically switched to None mode. Click on the banner message to acknowledge the emergency shutdown.
Holding minimum AP A Loss is being automatically controlled. Victus™ Simulation has automatically switched to Loss mode. Annular pressure is being held at minimum allowable AP, and Victus™ Simulation has stopped attempting to mitigate the loss. This occurs for losses detected in AP mode.
Holding minimum SBP A Loss is being automatically controlled. Victus™ Simulation has automatically switched to Loss mode. Surface backpressure is being held at minimum allowable SBP, and Victus™ Simulation has stopped attempting to mitigate the loss. This occurs for losses detected in SBP, Flow, or Connection mode.
Holding minimum SPP A Loss is being automatically controlled. Victus™ Simulation has automatically switched to Loss mode. Standpipe pressure is being held at minimum allowable SPP, and Victus™ Simulation has stopped attempting to mitigate the loss. This occurs for losses detected in SPP mode.
Influx Detected A Kick has been automatically detected. If automatic control is enabled, Victus™ Simulation will begin controlling the Kick. It is recommended that you apply the brake, maintain the current pump rate, and reduce pipe rotation to the minimum speed.
Influx Detected - Confirm!

A Kick has been automatically detected. However, automatic control is not enabled, and the Kick will not be controlled by Victus™ Simulation. Click on the banner message to acknowledge the Kick detection.
Influx out of well A Kick is being automatically controlled. The influx has finished circulating through well and is currently located at the surface.
Loss cannot be recovered A Loss is being automatically controlled. Victus™ Simulation has automatically switched to Loss mode. However, the loss could not be recovered because, in the attempt to mitigate the loss, the active chokes opened more than 80%.
Loss detected A Loss has been automatically detected. If automatic control is enabled, Victus™ Simulation will begin controlling the Loss.
Loss detected - Confirm!

A Loss has been automatically detected. However, automatic control is not enabled, and the Loss will not be controlled by Victus™ Simulation. Click on the banner message to acknowledge the Loss detection.
Loss Recovered - Confirm! A Loss is being automatically controlled. Flow out is now the same as flow in, so the loss appears to be recovered. Click on the banner message to confirm that the loss has been recovered.
Pump new mud (Recommended MW - XX.XX ppg) A Kick is being automatically controlled. If it is necessary to kill the well, you'll need to pump in a heavier mud. The recommended mud weight for the kill mud is specified in the banner message. To begin killing the well, select the kill mud from the Mud utility. See "Changing the pump fluid".
Reaching Circulation Pressure A Kick is being automatically controlled. Surface backpressure is being increased to control the kick, until it reaches the SBP set point.
