Overburden Stress Method

Overburden stress, or Lithostatic pressure is the pressure exerted by the weight of the overlying sediments, including weight of the contained fluids.

In the case of formation slowness or velocity data availability, empirical methods are used to convert log data into a “synthetic” density log. The Gardner method is an example of one of the industry's accepted relationships. Gardner empirical methods convert seismic, sonic, and check shot data into synthetic density (Gardner et. al. 1974):

  • ρ = α × Vβ,
  • where
    • ρ is the synthetic estimated density, in g/cm3.
    • α is an empirical coefficient, with a default value of α = 0.23.
    • β is an empirical coefficient, with a default value of β = 0.25.
    • V is the formation velocity, in ft/s.

Directly measured or estimated synthetic densities are averaged on the intervals, formation average interval density is further used for the overburden stress calculations.
