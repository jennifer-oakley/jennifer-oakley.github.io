About buckling

Drillstring buckling is a loss of stability that occurs due to a critical compression of axial loads. The axial load required to reach this critical threshold depends on the wellbore intervals and the geometry of the drillstring. There are two modes of drillstring buckling: sinusoidal and helical. In sinusoidal buckling, buckling occurs in an S-shaped configuration. In helical buckling, the buckling has a coiled shape. Drillstring buckling can occur in any wellbore, but the axial load required for buckling to occur depends on if the wellbore interval is vertical, horizontal, or curved.
