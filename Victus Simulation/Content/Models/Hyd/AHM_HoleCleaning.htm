About hole cleaning

Victus™ Simulation supports two mathematical models for predicting hole cleaning conditions in a well:

  • The basic hole cleaning model uses probability distribution functions to estimate downhole cleaning conditions under varying operating parameters. The primary outputs of the basic hole cleaning model are cuttings concentration, bed height, and the hole cleaning index. See "Basic hole cleaning model".
  • The advanced hole cleaning model captures fluid flow dynamics and the interaction of cuttings in the wellbore to predict the critical flow rate required for hole cleaning. See "Advanced hole cleaning model".

Basic hole cleaning model

Assumptions

The basic hole cleaning model makes the following assumptions:

  • Steady-state flow is modeled as one-dimensional flow.
  • There is no two-way interaction between the cuttings and the wellbore wall or work string.
  • Cuttings are spherical.
  • Fluid pressure is not affected by cuttings volume.

Features

There are three primary indicators that represent hole cleaning efficiency:

  • The concentration of the cuttings
  • The hole cleaning index
  • The height of the cuttings bed.

Hole cleaning efficiency depends on several factors, including:

  • The flow rate of drilling fluids
  • Hole angle
  • The rheological properties of the fluids
  • The rate of penetration
  • The rotary speed of the drillstring
  • The eccentricity of the drillstring

Advanced hole cleaning model

The advanced hole cleaning model captures fluid flow dynamics and the interaction of cuttings in the wellbore to predict the critical flow rate required for hole cleaning. At the critical flow rate, cuttings are transported with the mud and without cuttings beds building up in the wellbore. At lower flow rates, a cuttings bed will form, which can lead to pack-off.

Assumptions

The advanced hole cleaning model makes the following assumptions:

  • Steady-state flow is modeled as one-dimensional flow.
  • There is no two-way interaction between the cuttings and the wellbore wall or work string.
  • Cuttings are spherical.
  • Fluid pressure is not affected by cuttings volume.

Features

The advanced hole cleaning model includes the following features:

  • Uses four transport mechanisms for estimating the critical flow rate, based on the wellbore geometry and flow parameters. The available transport mechanisms include rolling, lifting, settling, and Kelvin-Helmholtz Instability.
  • Accounts for cuttings drag and lift coefficients.
  • Includes the effects of pipe rotation and eccentricity.
  • Primary outputs include cuttings concentration and bed height.
  • Validated against Clark's experimental data, Iyoho's experimental data, and experimental data from Zhang et all.
