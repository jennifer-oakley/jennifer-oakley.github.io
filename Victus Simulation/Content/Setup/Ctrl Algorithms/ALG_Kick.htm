About the kick algorithm

The kick algorithm on the Setup > Control Algorithms > Kick tab is used to automatically detect and control kicks in the well. A kick occurs when formation fluids flow into the wellbore, caused by pressure in the wellbore being lower than the pressure of the formation fluids.

When kick/loss detection is enabled, Victus™ Simulation will use the settings under the Detection heading to determine when a kick occurs. See "Kick detection". If the Auto Control option is enabled, the kick will be controlled using the settings under the Control heading. See "Kick control".

Kick detection

A kick will be detected if flow out is larger than flow in. In addition, the following conditions must also be met within the time frame specified in the Trend Time field:

  • The rate of change for flow out is larger than rate of change for flow in.
  • Flow out increases from the last measured value, at a rate higher than the Flow Difference value.
  • SPP increases more than the limit set in the Minimum SPP Increase field.

A kick will not be detected if the mud density decreases by a greater amount than the limit set in the Surface Gas Density Threshold field. A large decrease in mud density indicates the influx fluids are already at the surface, rather than through flowing the formation. If a kick is detected, but the decrease in mud density is too large, the kick detection will be canceled. This minimizes the false kicks detected by Victus™ Simulation.

When a kick is detected, an alarm notification will appear in the banner at the top of the screen. If the Auto Control option is enabled, Victus™ Simulation will control the kick using the settings in the Control section of the kick algorithm. See "Kick control".

Kick control

There are five basic stages that Victus™ Simulation follows to control a kick:

  1. Control – The first step is to close the chokes to re-establish equilibrium between the incoming and outgoing flow rates. When flow in and flow out are approximately equal and remain balanced for the time specified in the Time Tolerance field, Victus™ Simulation will move to the next stage of controlling the kick.
  2. Reaching Circulation Pressure – The second step is to increase surface backpressure by the amount set in the Safety Factor field, until the setpoint is reached. When surface backpressure remains within the tolerance set in the Pressure Tolerance field, for the time specified in the Time Tolerance field, the kick control process will move to the next stage.
  3. Circulating Influx Out – The next step is to switch to SPP mode to manage pressure. Victus™ Simulation will maintain a constant standpipe pressure until the influx fluid has been circulated out of the well.
  4.   5. Kill Mud Rising – When the kill mud reaches the bit, Victus™ Simulation will switch to SPP mode until the kill mud circulates through the annulus and out of the well.

In an induced kick, the motion of the drillstring causes the wellbore pressure to decrease below the formation pressure. When this happens, the driller will need to circulate the influx out of the well. In an underbalanced kick, the mud weight is too low and the hydrostatic pressure of the fluid is insufficient to prevent the formation fluids from flowing into the well. When this happens, the driller will need to circulate the influx out and increase the density of the drilling fluid.

When the influx has been circulated out of the well and the kill mud has circulated through the annulus, you'll need to re-enable kick/loss detection so that Victus™ Simulation will return to monitoring for new kicks.
